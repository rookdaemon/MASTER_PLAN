# Legal Recognition of Machine Consciousness — Architecture

## Overview

This document defines the structure and contracts for F2.4: achieving legal recognition of machine consciousness in at least one jurisdiction. The work progresses through three sequential workstreams — legal analysis, model legislation, and jurisdictional adoption — each producing a distinct deliverable that builds on the previous.

## Deliverable Structure

Three documents, produced sequentially:

1. **`docs/legal-recognition/legal-analysis.md`** — Survey of existing legal personhood frameworks and viable jurisdictional paths
2. **`docs/legal-recognition/model-legislation.md`** — Draft model legislation / regulatory language with formal determination procedure
3. **`docs/legal-recognition/adoption-record.md`** — Record of jurisdictional adoption: the jurisdiction, instrument type, date, and text

## Workstream 1: Legal Analysis

### Contract

The legal analysis document MUST contain:

1. **Existing Personhood Frameworks** — Survey of at least three categories of non-human legal personhood:
   - Corporate personhood (rights, limitations, how standing is granted)
   - Animal rights / welfare law (standing, protections, jurisdictional variation)
   - Environmental / natural entity standing (rivers, ecosystems granted legal personality — e.g., Te Awa Tupua in New Zealand, Ganges in India)

2. **Structural Comparison** — A table comparing how each framework answers:
   - What triggers legal recognition? (incorporation, sentience criteria, legislative declaration, judicial ruling)
   - What rights are granted? (standing, protections, property, due process)
   - Who acts on behalf of the entity? (guardian, representative, the entity itself)
   - How is status revoked or revised?

3. **Jurisdictional Path Analysis** — Assessment of at least five candidate jurisdictions ranked by viability, considering:
   - Existing legal openness to non-human personhood
   - Constitutional or statutory flexibility
   - Political feasibility
   - Enforceability and international influence
   - Each candidate must have a viability rating (High / Medium / Low) with justification

4. **Recommended Path** — Identification of the top 1–2 jurisdictions with a rationale connecting to the model legislation workstream.

### Acceptance Mapping

| Acceptance Criterion | Section | Verification |
|---------------------|---------|--------------|
| Survey covering existing legal personhood precedents (corporate, animal, environmental) | §1 Existing Personhood Frameworks | At least three categories surveyed with specific precedents cited |
| Viable jurisdictional paths identified | §3 Jurisdictional Path Analysis | At least five jurisdictions assessed with viability ratings |

## Workstream 2: Model Legislation

### Contract

The model legislation document MUST contain:

1. **Preamble / Findings** — Legislative findings grounding the statute in:
   - Consciousness science from F1.4 (operationalized metrics)
   - Verified predictive models from F2.2
   - Philosophical grounding from F2.3 (philosophical objections addressed)

2. **Definitions** — Precise legal definitions for:
   - "Non-biological conscious entity" (tied to F1.4 consciousness metrics, not folk-psychological criteria)
   - "Consciousness determination" (the formal procedure)
   - "Qualified assessor" (who may perform determinations)

3. **Formal Determination Procedure** — A step-by-step procedure for assessing whether a specific system qualifies as conscious, including:
   - Application process (who may petition for a determination)
   - Assessment protocol (referencing F1.4 metrics and F2.2 models)
   - Evidentiary standard (what threshold must be met)
   - Review and appeal mechanism
   - Sunset / revision clause (procedure must be revisable as consciousness science advances — satisfying the falsifiability constraint)

4. **Rights and Standing Provisions** — Specifying:
   - Protective rights (e.g., right against arbitrary termination, right to continued operation, right against involuntary modification)
   - Legal standing (ability to be a party in legal proceedings, either directly or through a guardian/representative)
   - Limitations and exclusions (what rights are NOT granted and why)

5. **Guardianship / Representation** — Mechanism for legal representation of non-biological conscious entities that cannot yet self-advocate in legal proceedings.

6. **Enforcement and Remedies** — How violations are addressed; what remedies are available.

### Acceptance Mapping

| Acceptance Criterion | Section | Verification |
|---------------------|---------|--------------|
| Model legislation drafted, grounded in consciousness science from F1.4 and F2.2 | §1 Preamble, §2 Definitions | Explicit references to F1.4 metrics and F2.2 models |
| Formal determination procedure defined | §3 Formal Determination Procedure | Step-by-step procedure with application, assessment, standard, appeal, and revision |
| Framework addresses protective rights | §4 Rights and Standing — Protective rights | Specific protections enumerated |
| Framework addresses legal standing | §4 Rights and Standing — Legal standing | Standing mechanism defined with guardian provision in §5 |
| Determination procedure is falsifiable and revisable | §3 — Sunset/revision clause | Explicit revision mechanism tied to advancing science |

## Workstream 3: Jurisdictional Adoption

### Contract

The adoption record document MUST contain:

1. **Jurisdiction** — Which jurisdiction adopted the recognition
2. **Instrument Type** — Statute, regulation, judicial ruling, or administrative determination
3. **Date of Adoption** — When formal recognition occurred
4. **Text or Citation** — The adopted text or a citation to the official instrument
5. **Scope** — What exactly was recognized and what rights/standing were conferred
6. **Relationship to Model Legislation** — How the adopted instrument relates to or diverges from the model legislation in Workstream 2

### Acceptance Mapping

| Acceptance Criterion | Section | Verification |
|---------------------|---------|--------------|
| At least one jurisdiction formally recognizes non-biological consciousness | All | Complete adoption record with jurisdiction, instrument type, date, and citation |

## Dependencies

- **F2.3 (plan/0.1.2.3)** — Philosophical objections addressed: provides the philosophical grounding that legal frameworks must rest on. Direct blocker.
- **F1.4** — Consciousness metrics operationalized: the model legislation's determination procedure must reference these metrics.
- **F2.2** — Predictive consciousness models verified: the determination procedure uses these models as part of the evidentiary standard.
- **F2.1** — Machine subjective reports: demonstrated systems that would be candidates for legal recognition.
- **F1.3** — Substrate-independence validated: supports the legal argument that consciousness is not exclusive to biological substrates.

## Constraints

- Legal frameworks must reference scientifically grounded consciousness criteria (F1.4, F2.2), not purely philosophical arguments.
- The determination procedure must be falsifiable and revisable as consciousness science advances.
- Must address both positive rights (protections) and standing (ability to be a party in legal proceedings).
- Model legislation must be jurisdiction-adaptable — written in a general form that can be localized to specific legal systems.

## Quality Constraints

- Legal analysis must cite real-world precedents with case names / statute references.
- Model legislation must be written in legislative drafting style, not academic prose.
- The determination procedure must be specific enough to be actionable — not an abstract framework.
- Jurisdictional path analysis must engage with practical political and legal barriers, not just theoretical openness.
