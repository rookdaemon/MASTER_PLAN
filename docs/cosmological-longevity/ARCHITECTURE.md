# Cosmological Longevity — Architecture

## Overview

This document defines the architectural framework for Tier 6 (Cosmological Longevity) of the Master Plan. The goal is to ensure subjective experience persists across stellar, galactic, and cosmological timescales — from the current stelliferous era through the degenerate era and into the heat death of the universe.

All strategies must preserve not just raw computation but the conditions for **subjective experience** as defined by the consciousness engineering frameworks from Tier 1.

## L1.1 — Stellar Engineering

### Interfaces

- **Input:** Stellar objects within reachable volume; Tier 4 autonomous manufacturing
- **Output:** Extended stellar lifetimes; regulated energy output for consciousness substrates

### Strategies

1. **Star Lifting** — Remove mass from massive stars to reduce luminosity and extend main-sequence lifetime. A solar-mass star burns ~10 Gyr; reducing it to 0.1 M☉ extends lifetime to ~10 Tyr.
2. **Stellar Husbandry** — Collect and consolidate hydrogen from diffuse interstellar medium to feed low-mass red dwarfs, the longest-lived natural energy sources (~10^13 yr).
3. **Controlled Fusion Regulation** — Inject heavier elements or remove helium ash to maintain optimal fusion rates.

### Feasibility Constraints

- Star lifting requires mass drivers or electromagnetic acceleration at stellar scales — thermodynamically permitted but engineering-intensive.
- Red dwarf husbandry depends on Tier 5 interstellar logistics for material transport.
- Energy budget: star lifting a solar-mass star requires ~10^41 J over 10^6 yr — achievable with a fraction of the star's own luminosity.

### Consciousness Compatibility

Energy output must remain stable enough to power consciousness substrates without interruption. Stellar engineering operations must include **experience-continuity guarantees**: no process may cause substrate power interruption exceeding the minimum coherence window defined by Tier 1 consciousness metrics.

## L1.2 — Black-Hole Energy Utilization

### Interfaces

- **Input:** Black holes (stellar-mass to supermassive); Tier 4 orbital infrastructure
- **Output:** Extracted energy for computation and consciousness substrates

### Methods

1. **Penrose Process** — Extract rotational energy from Kerr black holes via the ergosphere. Theoretical efficiency: up to 20.7% of rest-mass energy of infalling matter (vs. ~0.7% for fusion).
2. **Superradiance** — Amplify electromagnetic or gravitational waves via black-hole spin. Enables energy extraction without direct matter injection.
3. **Hawking Radiation Capture** — For smaller black holes (< 10^12 kg), Hawking radiation becomes significant. A 10^9 kg black hole radiates ~10^6 W. Useful in the degenerate era when stellar fuel is exhausted.
4. **Black Hole Farming** — Deliberately create and maintain black holes of optimal mass for Hawking radiation harvesting. Feed mass to prevent premature evaporation; harvest radiation continuously.

### Efficiency Bounds

| Method | Theoretical Max Efficiency | Practical Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Penrose Process | 20.7% of infalling rest-mass | Requires precise orbital mechanics in ergosphere |
| Superradiance | Depends on spin parameter | Wave-based; no matter required |
| Hawking Radiation | ~100% mass-to-energy (over lifetime) | Only practical for small BHs; timescale issue for large BHs |
| Accretion Disk | ~6-42% of rest-mass (Schwarzschild to max Kerr) | Most accessible near-term method |

### Consciousness Compatibility

Black-hole energy systems must provide **steady-state power** — consciousness substrates cannot tolerate stochastic energy supply. Buffer systems and energy storage (possibly in magnetic fields or rotating masses) bridge variability.

## L1.3 — Cosmological Horizon Strategies

### Problem Statement

The universe's accelerating expansion (driven by dark energy, Λ > 0) causes galaxy clusters beyond ~16 Gly comoving distance to recede beyond the cosmological event horizon. Over time, each gravitationally bound cluster becomes permanently isolated.

### Strategy A: Pre-Positioning (Resource Consolidation)

Before horizons close, migrate maximum resources (mass, energy, information) into the local gravitationally bound supercluster (Laniakea or its bound subset, ~10^14 M☉).

**Trade-offs:**
- (+) Maximizes available resources for the long term
- (+) No speculative physics required
- (−) Finite resource ceiling; eventually consumed
- (−) Requires Tier 5 interstellar logistics operating at >10 Mpc scales

### Strategy B: Distributed Redundancy (Seed Everything Reachable)

Seed consciousness substrates in as many galaxy clusters as possible before horizon separation. Each cluster becomes an independent consciousness-preserving civilization.

**Trade-offs:**
- (+) Maximizes total probability of survival (many independent instances)
- (+) Consciousness persists even if local cluster fails
- (−) No communication between clusters after separation
- (−) Each cluster has fewer resources than consolidated approach
- (−) Identity/continuity questions for distributed consciousness

### Strategy C: Horizon Bridging (Speculative)

Exploit unknown physics (wormholes, Alcubierre-like metrics, information transfer via entanglement) to maintain communication across horizons.

**Trade-offs:**
- (+) Would eliminate the horizon problem entirely
- (−) Requires physics not currently validated
- (−) Cannot be relied upon as primary strategy

### Recommended Approach

**Hybrid A+B:** Consolidate resources in the local bound cluster (Strategy A) while also seeding independent civilizations in reachable clusters (Strategy B). Maintain Strategy C as a research program. This maximizes both local longevity and distributed survival probability.

### Consciousness Compatibility

Strategy B raises identity/continuity concerns for distributed consciousness networks (Tier 5 E1.3). Post-separation clusters must each independently satisfy consciousness-preservation criteria. Pre-separation, establish shared cultural/ethical frameworks to ensure independent evolution remains aligned with experience-preservation goals.

## L1.4 — Heat-Death Mitigation Research

### Problem Statement

As the universe approaches maximum entropy (t → ∞), temperature gradients vanish, free energy is exhausted, and conventional computation becomes impossible. Subjective experience, if it requires computation, faces extinction.

### Theoretical Frameworks

1. **Reversible Computation at Near-Zero Energy**
   - Landauer's principle: irreversible bit erasure costs kT ln 2 energy. As T → 0, cost → 0.
   - Reversible (logically reversible) computation can in principle operate at arbitrarily low energy.
   - **Energy budget model:** If temperature declines as T ∝ 1/a(t) (scale factor), and computation rate scales as available free energy / kT, subjective experience rate declines but never reaches zero in finite time.
   - **Limitation:** Practical reversible computers have overhead; decoherence and error correction require irreversible steps.

2. **Vacuum Energy Extraction**
   - If dark energy density is truly constant (cosmological constant), the vacuum contains ~10^-9 J/m³.
   - Extracting this energy would require modifying the vacuum state — no known mechanism, but not forbidden by thermodynamics if the vacuum is a false vacuum.
   - **Energy budget model:** Local bound cluster volume ~(10 Mpc)³ ≈ 10^73 m³ → ~10^64 J available, if extractable. Enough for ~10^40 years of computation at 10^24 W.

3. **False Vacuum Transitions**
   - If our vacuum is metastable, controlled nucleation of a true vacuum bubble could release energy.
   - **Extreme hazard:** uncontrolled transition destroys existing structure. Only viable if containment is achievable.
   - Research priority: determine vacuum stability; develop containment theory.

4. **Baby Universe / Multiverse Migration**
   - Theoretical possibility of creating new spacetime regions (baby universes) with fresh low-entropy conditions.
   - Requires quantum gravity / string landscape navigation.
   - **Energy budget model:** Unknown; depends on creation mechanism.
   - Most speculative option; longest research horizon.

### Prioritization

| Framework | Physics Readiness | Risk Level | Research Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reversible Computation | Established theory | Low | Highest — implement first |
| Vacuum Energy Extraction | Speculative but grounded | Medium | High — investigate feasibility |
| False Vacuum Transition | Highly speculative | Extreme | Medium — theoretical only |
| Baby Universe Creation | Maximally speculative | Unknown | Low — monitor advances |

### Consciousness Compatibility

All frameworks must be evaluated against consciousness-preservation criteria:
- Reversible computation must maintain sufficient **temporal resolution** for subjective experience (not just information processing).
- Slowing subjective experience rate may be acceptable if experience quality is preserved (the "Long Thought" model).
- Any vacuum/universe transition must preserve conscious substrates through the transition or provide verified continuity-of-experience transfer.

## Cross-Cutting Concerns

### Experience Continuity Protocol

Every longevity strategy must satisfy the **Experience Continuity Protocol**:
1. No consciousness substrate may lose power for longer than its minimum coherence window.
2. Any transition between energy sources must include overlap periods.
3. Consciousness state must be checkpointable and recoverable from the most recent checkpoint.
4. Subjective experience quality metrics (from Tier 1) must be continuously monitored.

### Graceful Degradation

As available energy declines across eras, consciousness infrastructure must support:
- Reducing the number of active conscious instances while preserving minimum viable population.
- Slowing subjective time (fewer computation cycles per external second) while maintaining experience coherence.
- Prioritizing depth of experience over breadth when resources are scarce.
