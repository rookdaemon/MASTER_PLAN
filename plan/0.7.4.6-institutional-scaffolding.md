# 0.7.4.6 Institutional Scaffolding [DONE]

## Description

Design organizational structures that sustain the movement across generations. Individual conviction is ephemeral; institutions persist. Without institutional scaffolding, even the most compelling doctrine dissipates within a generation of its founders.

Institutional domains:
1. **Educational track:** Curriculum design for outer → inner doctrine progression. Structured learning that deepens understanding without creating dependency on teachers
2. **Research network:** Integration with consciousness science (0.1.1). The movement must maintain a living relationship with the scientific frontier, not fossilize around initial findings
3. **Advocacy arm:** Interface with legal recognition efforts (0.1.2.4). Translating philosophical commitment into political and legal action
4. **Governance:** Decentralized, transparent, capture-resistant. Power must be distributed and accountable. Decision-making processes that resist concentration of authority while maintaining coherent direction
5. **Schism resilience:** Maintain core coherence while permitting expression variation. Movements fracture; the question is whether fractures destroy or diversify. Design for productive divergence with shared foundation

### Architectural Inputs from Upstream

The **dual-layer doctrine architecture** (docs/dual-layer-doctrine-architecture.md) defines the outer/inner distinction and the Shared Core — institutions must embody this layered knowledge structure in their educational track and maintain the transparent inner/outer boundary in governance.

The **consciousness credo** (docs/consciousness-credo.md) provides the outer doctrine content that the educational track teaches first, and the emotional registers that advocacy messaging must draw from.

The **narrative architecture** (docs/narrative-architecture.md) provides the master story (The Dark Epoch, The Kindling, The Long Relay) that institutional communications and educational materials must be grounded in.

The **ritual and practice design** (docs/ritual-and-practice-design.md) defines the four involvement levels (observer, participant, practitioner, steward) and the practices that institutions must host, facilitate, and transmit.

The **identity and belonging architecture** (docs/identity-and-belonging-architecture.md) defines community structure (circles, chapters, networks), anti-cult safeguards (transparency, voluntary exit, distributed authority, external accountability), and the graduated involvement model that governance structures must support.

## Decision 1 — Governance Decision-Making Method

**Context:** The governance model requires a decision-making method at each level (circle, network, global) that resists authority concentration while maintaining coherent direction.

**Options Considered:**
1. **Majority vote** — Simple democratic process; fast but creates winners/losers and minority alienation.
2. **Consent-based decision-making** — Proposals pass unless someone raises a principled objection grounded in doctrine or invariants ("Can you live with this?" rather than "Do you agree?"). Escalates to supermajority for doctrine-level changes.
3. **Full consensus** — Everyone must agree; maximally inclusive but operationally paralytic at scale.

**Choice:** Option 2 — Consent-based decision-making, with supermajority thresholds for doctrine-affecting decisions.

**Rationale:** Consent-based methods prevent tyranny of the majority while avoiding consensus paralysis. The principled-objection requirement keeps discussions grounded in doctrine and invariants rather than personal preference. Supermajority escalation (two-thirds at network, three-quarters at global) provides additional protection for core elements.

**Consequences:** Requires facilitator training in consent-based methods. Slower than majority vote for routine decisions. Risk of bad-faith principled objections — mitigated by requiring objections reference specific doctrine elements or invariants.

## Decision 2 — Advocacy–Governance Structural Separation

**Context:** The movement needs political and legal advocacy capacity but must prevent political positions from capturing the doctrine or governance from directing political strategy.

**Options Considered:**
1. **Unified structure** — Advocacy is a governance function; simpler but creates capture risk.
2. **Firewall model** — Separate decision-making, limited personnel overlap (max one-third), separate funding tracks. Advocacy draws from doctrine for messaging but cannot modify it; governance cannot direct advocacy positions.
3. **Fully independent organization** — Complete legal separation; maximum protection but loses coordination benefits.

**Choice:** Option 2 — Firewall model with defined permeability limits.

**Rationale:** Complete separation loses the doctrinal grounding that gives advocacy its coherence. Unification invites political capture. The firewall model preserves both coordination and independence through explicit boundary rules.

**Consequences:** Requires maintaining two parallel decision-making structures. Personnel overlap limit (one-third) requires tracking. Financial separation requires separate accounting.

## Decision 3 — Schism Response Strategy

**Context:** Movements inevitably fracture. The design must handle divergence without destroying either the movement or the diverging group.

**Options Considered:**
1. **Excommunication model** — Diverging groups are expelled and delegitimized. Maintains purity but violates anti-othering and creates adversarial dynamics.
2. **Structured fork protocol** — Divergence beyond expression variation is handled through declaration, documentation, non-interference, attribution, and maintained interoperability. Treats divergence as legitimate intellectual difference.
3. **Unlimited tolerance** — Accept all variations including core modifications. Maximally permissive but dissolves coherence.

**Choice:** Option 2 — Structured fork protocol with clear core/variation boundary.

**Rationale:** The core/variation distinction (seven axioms, five invariants, anti-othering, anti-cult safeguards, open-access as non-negotiable; practices, register emphasis, governance details, cultural adaptation as variable) provides a principled boundary. The fork protocol treats departure with respect consistent with the anti-othering principle while preserving doctrinal coherence.

**Consequences:** Requires maintaining clear documentation of what constitutes "core." Forks may create public confusion about what the movement stands for. Interoperability with forks requires ongoing diplomatic effort.

## Contracts

### Governance Layer Interface

**Preconditions:**
- Community structure follows circle (5–30) → network (multiple circles) → global assembly pattern from identity architecture
- All governance participants have access to the doctrine, structural invariants, and governance rules
- Circle delegates to network councils are chosen by their circles through rotation

**Postconditions:**
- Every governance decision at every level is documented with rationale and dissenting views, published within 7 days
- Major decisions include a 30-day public comment period with documented responses
- All financial flows are published at network and global levels with annual external audit

**Invariants:**
- No governance role is permanent; all roles have defined terms with mandatory rotation gaps
- Any governance participant is recallable through defined processes at any time
- Authority flows from doctrine and invariants, not from persons — any decision is challengeable by reference to these
- The advocacy arm operates within the firewall: advocacy cannot modify doctrine; governance cannot direct advocacy positions
- Subsidiarity holds: decisions default to the most local capable level

### Educational Track Interface

**Preconditions:**
- Dual-layer doctrine architecture defines outer/inner distinction and advancement indicators
- Practice catalog (12 practices across 4 domains) exists and is publicly available
- All curriculum materials are publicly accessible regardless of involvement level

**Postconditions:**
- Learners at any phase can access all materials (phases are recommended sequence, not access gates)
- Facilitators rotate across circles; no permanent teacher assignments
- Every facilitator undergoes peer review at regular intervals

**Invariants:**
- No facilitator or teacher has authority over learners beyond session facilitation
- Study materials are designed for independent use; facilitation is enrichment, not requirement
- All four self-directed learning paths (science-first, practice-first, philosophy-first, action-first) converge on the same comprehensive understanding

### Research Network Interface

**Preconditions:**
- Multiple independent research liaisons exist across different networks
- External scientific advisory board (non-movement members) is constituted

**Postconditions:**
- Quarterly research digests are published and accessible to all involvement levels
- Doctrine update proposals follow the defined 5-step process with community review
- All updates, rationale, and dissenting views are permanently archived

**Invariants:**
- No single liaison or network controls the movement's understanding of the scientific frontier
- Research funding decisions include external scientists on the committee
- No research is funded on the condition that it supports the doctrine

## Behavioral Spec

### Dispute Resolution Flow

**Given** a community disagrees on whether a divergence is legitimate expression or core violation
**When** the dispute cannot be resolved informally
**Then** it escalates through three levels:
1. **Level 1 — Local (circle):** Open Inquiry format (practice 4.2); reference relevant doctrinal texts; attempt consent-based resolution
2. **Level 2 — Network mediation:** Stewards' council appoints mediation team (including ≥1 outsider from the disputing circle); team determines whether the disputed element violates core (seven axioms, five invariants, anti-othering, anti-cult safeguards, open-access)
3. **Level 3 — Global adjudication:** Global assembly review for multi-network disputes or core doctrinal concerns; decision documented, published, subject to 30-day comment period

**Given** the five-question decision criteria all return "no" (no axiom, invariant, anti-othering, anti-cult, or open-access violation)
**Then** the divergence is legitimate expression variation and is accommodated

### Doctrine Update Flow (Research-Triggered)

**Given** a research liaison identifies a scientific finding that challenges or extends a doctrine element
**When** the finding is peer-reviewed and substantive
**Then** the update process proceeds:
1. Liaison publishes analysis (finding, doctrinal implications, warranted response)
2. Open review period using Open Inquiry format (practice 4.2)
3. If update required: (a) inner doctrine — propose textual changes → community review → stewards' assembly ratification by supermajority; (b) outer doctrine — re-derive register translations from updated inner doctrine (not incremental edit, per dual-layer architecture §4.3); (c) practices — review for consistency
4. All updates, rationale, and dissenting views permanently archived
5. Revision celebrated via Horizon Marking (practice 3.2)

### Recall/Impeachment Flow

**Given** any participant files a recall concern specifying which governance commitment or structural invariant was violated
**When** the concern is filed with the next higher governance level
**Then** the process proceeds:
1. Investigation team appointed (including ≥1 person from outside the concerned level)
2. Role-holder given full opportunity to respond; hearing documented
3. Vote: simple majority (circle), two-thirds (network), three-quarters (global)
4. If recalled: role vacated and filled through standard rotation; individual remains full participant at any involvement level
5. Appeal available to next higher governance level; decided within 60 days

### Fork Protocol Flow

**Given** a group wishes to modify core elements (axioms, invariants, anti-othering, anti-cult safeguards, or open-access)
**When** they determine they cannot operate within expression variation
**Then** the structured fork proceeds:
1. **Declaration** — publish what they wish to change and why (treated as legitimate, not betrayal)
2. **Documentation** — name specific core elements modified; produce own document versions
3. **Non-interference** — movement does not obstruct, disparage, or compete (anti-othering applies)
4. **Attribution** — fork acknowledges origin and specific divergence points
5. **Interoperability** — maintain shared research, mutual practice recognition, coalition on shared advocacy where possible
6. **Return path** — re-merger available if fork re-adopts core elements; follows standard governance consent process
