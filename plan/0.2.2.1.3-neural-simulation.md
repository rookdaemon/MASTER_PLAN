# 0.2.2.1.3 Neural Simulation [DONE]

## Description

Build computational infrastructure capable of simulating a complete biological brain at the required fidelity in real-time or faster.

### Key challenges
- Computational scale: a molecular-level simulation of a human brain may require exascale or beyond
- Real-time constraint: subjective experience likely requires temporal dynamics to proceed at biological speed or faster
- Numerical stability over extended simulation runs (years, decades)
- Energy efficiency: the biological brain operates on ~20W; the simulation must be practically sustainable
- Integration with enduring substrates (S1) for long-term operation

### Deliverable

An architecture document (`docs/neural-simulation-architecture.md`) specifying the simulation engine design, compute requirements, and integration points with scan data (0.2.2.1.2) and enduring substrates (0.2.1).

## Acceptance Criteria
- Simulation algorithm selected and justified for the fidelity level specified by 0.2.2.1.1 (e.g. compartmental models, spiking networks, molecular dynamics, or hybrid approaches)
- Time-stepping scheme specified with demonstrated stability bounds; error accumulation formally bounded for simulation durations of at least 1 simulated year
- Parallelization and spatial decomposition strategy defined for ~86 billion neurons at the required fidelity, with concrete FLOPS and memory estimates
- Scan data ingestion pipeline specified: how whole-brain scan output (from 0.2.2.1.2) initializes the simulation state (neuron parameters, connectivity, dynamic state)
- Real-time or faster execution demonstrated on at least one biologically plausible brain model (any organism scale), with a validated scaling path to human-scale
- Energy budget estimated and compared to practical sustainability thresholds; optimization strategies identified if initial estimates exceed feasible limits
- Hardware target specified: either enduring substrate hardware (from 0.2.1) or conventional compute with a documented migration path to enduring substrates
- Simulated neural dynamics validated against known biological benchmarks (e.g. oscillatory rhythms, resting-state patterns, stimulus-response characteristics)
