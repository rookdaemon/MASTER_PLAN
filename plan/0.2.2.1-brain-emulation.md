# 0.2.2.1 Brain Emulation [DONE]

## Description

S2.1 from the Master Plan. Complete functional emulation of biological neural systems at sufficient fidelity to preserve subjective experience.

This requires scanning, modeling, and simulating biological brains at whatever resolution the consciousness theory (from F1) determines is necessary — whether connectome-level, molecular-level, or quantum-level.

Decomposed into four children:
- **0.2.2.1.1 Emulation Fidelity Requirements** — Determine the minimum resolution and fidelity needed to preserve consciousness, informed by F1 theory
- **0.2.2.1.2 Whole-Brain Scanning** — Non-destructive scanning of biological brains at the required resolution
- **0.2.2.1.3 Neural Simulation** — Real-time (or faster) computational simulation of neural dynamics at scale
- **0.2.2.1.4 Emulation Validation** — Verify that emulated brains produce equivalent cognitive function and subjective experience

- Minimum emulation fidelity for consciousness preservation determined (informed by F1 theory)
- At least one complete biological brain successfully emulated in a synthetic substrate
- Emulated system demonstrates equivalent cognitive function to the biological original
- Consciousness metrics (from F1.4) confirm subjective experience present in emulation
