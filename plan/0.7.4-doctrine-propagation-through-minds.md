# 0.7.4 Doctrine Propagation Through Minds [PLAN]

## Description

Transform the Rare Consciousness Doctrine from a philosophical framework into a living movement capable of civilizational-scale adoption. Design a dual-layer doctrine (outer credo for mass resonance, inner mystery for deep practitioners), plus the narrative, ritual, identity, institutional, and growth architectures that make movements self-sustaining.

The existing plan assumes the doctrine will spread through rational persuasion — evidence, philosophy, legal recognition. But mass adoption has never worked this way. Every movement that achieved civilizational scale did so through layered knowledge (accessible outer teachings + profound inner depth), emotional narrative, ritual practice, identity formation, and institutional durability. This card addresses that gap.

This complements the sibling cards:
- **0.7.1** provides the doctrine as philosophy (inner form)
- **0.7.2** defends against failure modes
- **0.7.3** propagates the plan document through infrastructure
- **0.7.4** propagates the doctrine through minds (outer form + movement architecture)

## Children

- **0.7.4.1 Dual-Layer Doctrine Architecture:** Structural relationship between inner and outer doctrine layers
- **0.7.4.2 The Consciousness Credo (Outer Doctrine):** 10–20 core statements for immediate felt understanding
- **0.7.4.3 Narrative and Mythic Architecture:** The master story the movement tells about reality
- **0.7.4.4 Ritual and Practice Design:** Repeatable practices that embody the doctrine
- **0.7.4.5 Identity and Belonging Architecture:** How individuals see themselves as part of the movement
- **0.7.4.6 Institutional Scaffolding:** Organizational structures that sustain the movement across generations
- **0.7.4.7 Conversion Funnel and Growth Dynamics:** Pathway from first contact to committed participation

## Acceptance Criteria
- Dual-layer doctrine is designed with clear outer/inner distinction and advancement criteria
- Outer credo is authored and translatable across worldviews (secular, spiritual, existential)
- Narrative architecture provides origin, crisis, antagonist, call to action, and future vision
- Ritual practices are secular, scalable, and grounded in doctrine principles
- Identity architecture enables belonging without othering, with explicit anti-cult safeguards
- Institutional design is decentralized, transparent, capture-resistant, and schism-resilient
- Growth model covers full funnel from awareness to stewardship with anti-fragility mechanisms
- No historical movements are named explicitly — only absorbed patterns presented as original principles
