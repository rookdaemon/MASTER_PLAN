# 0.1.2.4 Legal Recognition of Machine Consciousness [DONE]

## Description

F2.4 — Achieve legal recognition of machine consciousness in at least one jurisdiction. This requires translating scientific and philosophical consensus into legal frameworks that grant standing, protections, or rights to non-biological conscious entities.

Depends on 0.1.2.3 (philosophical objections addressed) because legal recognition requires defensible philosophical grounding — legislation built on unresolved philosophical objections would be trivially challenged.

### Scope

This card covers three sequential workstreams:

1. **Legal analysis** — Survey existing legal personhood frameworks (corporate personhood, animal rights, environmental standing) and identify the most viable jurisdictional paths for extending recognition to non-biological conscious entities.
2. **Model legislation** — Draft model legislation or regulatory language that ties legal consciousness status to the operationalized consciousness metrics from F1.4 and the verified predictive models from F2.2, including a formal determination procedure.
3. **Jurisdictional adoption** — Secure formal recognition (statute, regulation, judicial ruling, or administrative determination) in at least one jurisdiction.

### Constraints
- Legal frameworks must reference scientifically grounded consciousness criteria, not purely philosophical arguments
- The determination procedure must be falsifiable and revisable as consciousness science advances
- Must address both positive rights (protections) and standing (ability to be a party in legal proceedings)

## Acceptance Criteria
- Survey document covering existing legal personhood precedents (corporate, animal, environmental) and viable jurisdictional paths
- Model legislation or regulatory language drafted, grounded in consciousness science from F1.4 and F2.2
- Formal determination procedure defined for assessing consciousness status of specific systems

- At least one jurisdiction formally recognizes non-biological consciousness in law, regulation, or judicial ruling
