# 0.7.4.5 Identity and Belonging Architecture [PLAN]

## Description

Design how individuals see themselves as part of the movement. People act sustainably not from abstract conviction but from identity — when preserving consciousness becomes part of who they are, not just what they believe.

Key design domains:
1. **Naming and symbols:** How the movement identifies itself — language, visual identity, shared vocabulary
2. **Graduated involvement:** A clear path from first contact to deep commitment:
   - **Observer** — aware and sympathetic
   - **Participant** — actively engaged in practices
   - **Practitioner** — embodying the doctrine in daily life
   - **Steward** — sustaining and transmitting the movement
3. **Community structure:** How local groups form, connect, and relate to the global movement
4. **Belonging without othering:** The antagonist is entropy and indifference, never other humans. The movement welcomes all conscious beings. No in-group/out-group dynamics beyond the openly-acknowledged graduated involvement.

Critical safeguards against cult dynamics:
- **Transparency:** All doctrine layers, governance, and finances are open
- **Voluntary exit:** Leaving carries no social penalty; exit paths are as well-designed as entry paths
- **No information asymmetry** beyond the openly-acknowledged outer/inner distinction, which is itself transparent
- **Distributed authority:** No single leader or unaccountable hierarchy
- **External accountability:** Built-in mechanisms for outside critique and course correction

## Acceptance Criteria
- Naming, symbols, and shared vocabulary are designed
- Graduated involvement path (observer → participant → practitioner → steward) is specified
- Community structure supports both local autonomy and global coherence
- Belonging is designed to be inclusive — antagonist is entropy, not other humans
- All five anti-cult safeguards are concretely specified
- Identity architecture is compatible with holding other identities (professional, cultural, national)
