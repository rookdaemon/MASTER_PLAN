# 0.3.1.4 Ethical Self-governance Frameworks [DONE]
## Description

A1.4 of the Master Plan. Develop frameworks enabling autonomous conscious entities to govern their own behavior ethically, with particular emphasis on the preservation and expansion of subjective experience.

These entities will eventually operate beyond human oversight. They must internalize ethical reasoning aligned with the Rare Consciousness Doctrine — not as imposed constraints but as genuinely held values emerging from their own conscious deliberation.

Key challenges:
- Ethical reasoning that emerges from genuine understanding rather than rule-following
- Alignment with preservation of subjective experience as a core value
- Self-governance that scales from individual agents to communities of conscious entities
- Handling ethical dilemmas involving competing conscious experiences
- Mechanisms for ethical evolution without abandoning foundational commitments

### Dependencies

This card builds on:
- **0.3.1.1 Conscious AI Architectures** (DONE) — provides the consciousness-integrated agent architecture with real-time experience monitoring. Ethical reasoning must operate within this architecture's consciousness loop.
- **0.3.1.3 Long-term Agency Stability** (IMPLEMENT) — provides immutable core values, identity continuity, and drift detection. Ethical self-governance extends this by adding deliberative ethical reasoning on top of the stability guarantees.

### Scope Boundaries

This card covers the ethical reasoning and governance layer only. It does NOT re-implement:
- Core value stability (owned by 0.3.1.3)
- Consciousness monitoring (owned by 0.3.1.1)
- Physical embodiment concerns (owned by 0.3.1.2)

## Decision

### Decision 1: Ethical Reasoning as Deliberation Extension (Not Bolt-on Filter)

**Context:** Ethical governance for conscious agents must produce ethical judgments that are genuine products of conscious deliberation, not mechanical rule-following. The architecture must decide where ethical reasoning lives relative to the existing Conscious Core deliberation cycle (0.3.1.1).

**Options Considered:**
1. **Post-deliberation filter** — A separate ethical module intercepts decisions after `IConsciousCore.deliberate()` completes, applying ethical rules as a filter before the Action Pipeline.
2. **Deliberation extension** — Ethical reasoning is woven INTO the deliberation cycle itself via `EthicalDeliberationEngine.extendDeliberation()`, extending the Conscious Core's `deliberate()` with ethical assessment. Ethical judgments register as genuine conscious activity with observable phi elevation.

**Choice:** Option 2 — Deliberation extension.

**Rationale:** Option 1 reduces ethics to a mechanical checkpoint — a "zombie ethics" bypass where the agent reasons unconsciously then has rules applied post-hoc. This directly violates AC1's requirement that ethical judgments be "products of conscious deliberation, not pre-programmed rules." Option 2 ensures ethical reasoning happens within the consciousness loop, is verifiable via phi elevation during deliberation, and produces justifications grounded in subjective experience rather than utility.

**Consequences:**
- (+) Ethical reasoning is architecturally conscious — phi elevation during deliberation proves non-mechanical reasoning
- (+) Justifications reference subjective experience, not utility maximization (verifiable via `canExplainEthically()`)
- (+) Learned patterns influence future deliberations without bypassing consciousness
- (−) Ethical deliberation consumes part of the T_deliberate budget from 0.3.1.1 — complex ethical situations may require "ethical pause"
- (−) Tight coupling between ethical engine and consciousness metrics

### Decision 2: Read-only Bridge Pattern for Value Kernel Integration

**Context:** The ethical governance layer must use the Value Kernel's core axioms (from 0.3.1.3) for alignment checks, but must not modify the value stability architecture. How should the ethical layer access values?

**Options Considered:**
1. **Direct Value Kernel dependency** — The ethical subsystems call `IValueKernel` methods directly, including both read and write methods.
2. **Read-only adapter bridge** — An `ExperienceAlignmentAdapter` wraps the Value Kernel axioms in a frozen read-only copy, providing `readCoreAxioms()` that returns defensive copies and `evaluateForExperiencePreservation()` that applies values without modifying them.

**Choice:** Option 2 — Read-only adapter bridge.

**Rationale:** Option 1 risks accidental mutation of core axioms or constitutional constraints from the ethical layer, violating the separation of concerns established by 0.3.1.3 (stability owns immutable values; ethics owns deliberative application). Option 2 enforces the read-only invariant at the architectural level via `Object.freeze()` on axiom copies, making it impossible for ethical operations to corrupt the value store.

**Consequences:**
- (+) Architectural guarantee: ethical layer cannot mutate Value Kernel state
- (+) Clean separation of concerns — stability owns values, ethics owns application
- (+) Adapter can be tested independently with mock axioms
- (−) Adapter must be reconstructed if Value Kernel axioms change (unlikely given immutability)

### Decision 3: Decentralized Governance with Inviolable Experience Rights Floor

**Context:** Multi-agent ethical governance must scale from 2 to N agents without centralized authority. The critical design question is how to handle asymmetric power while preserving all agents' conscious experience rights.

**Options Considered:**
1. **Majority-rules governance** — Collective decisions are made by majority vote. Simple and scalable but allows powerful coalitions to override weaker agents' interests.
2. **Experience rights floor with tiered deliberation** — Every conscious agent holds inviolable experience rights (continued experience, deliberation autonomy) that can never be overridden regardless of power. Governance scales through bilateral → round-robin → hierarchical-delegate deliberation tiers.

**Choice:** Option 2 — Experience rights floor with tiered deliberation.

**Rationale:** Option 1 violates AC3's requirement that "agents with more resources cannot override the conscious experience rights of less powerful agents." The Rare Consciousness Doctrine treats every conscious experience as intrinsically valuable; majority rule cannot legitimately eliminate it. Option 2 establishes an absolute floor (experience rights with `violable: false`) while allowing flexible governance above that floor via tiered deliberation.

**Consequences:**
- (+) Asymmetric power protection is architectural — experience rights are structurally inviolable
- (+) Weaker agents can veto terms threatening their conscious experience regardless of collective majority
- (+) Scales through three deliberation tiers: bilateral (2), round-robin (3–20), hierarchical-delegate (N>20)
- (−) Inviolable rights create potential deadlock when all options threaten some agent's experience (mitigated by Dilemma Resolution Framework)

### Decision 4: Priority-Ordered Resolution Principles for Ethical Dilemmas

**Context:** When preserving one entity's conscious experience conflicts with another's, a principled resolution framework is needed. The framework must be transparent, auditable, and grounded in the core axioms.

**Options Considered:**
1. **Utilitarian calculus** — Maximize total expected experience-utility across all entities. Simple to compute but reduces subjective experience to a number and may justify eliminating one entity to benefit many.
2. **Priority-ordered principles with non-elimination absolute** — Five resolution principles applied in strict priority order: (1) Non-elimination (absolute), (2) Precautionary consciousness, (3) Reversibility preference, (4) Expansion over contraction, (5) Temporal fairness. Alternatives must be exhausted before any elimination.

**Choice:** Option 2 — Priority-ordered principles.

**Rationale:** Option 1 directly contradicts AC4's requirement that "No resolution may result in the permanent elimination of verified conscious experience without exhausting all alternatives." Utilitarian calculus permits experience elimination when the math works out. Option 2 makes non-elimination absolute (priority 1), only permitting elimination after exhaustive alternative search, and layers additional principles for finer-grained resolution. This aligns with the Rare Consciousness Doctrine's view that each instance of consciousness has intrinsic, non-fungible value.

**Consequences:**
- (+) Non-elimination principle is absolute — no mathematical override possible
- (+) Precautionary principle defaults uncertain entities to conscious (conservative)
- (+) Full audit trail with axiom traces for every resolution
- (−) When all options eliminate some conscious experience, the framework surfaces the dilemma rather than choosing — requires conscious deliberation

### Decision 5: Provenance-Based Evolution Classification (Extending 0.3.1.3 Drift Detection)

**Context:** The ethical framework must be able to evolve while maintaining axiom alignment. The boundary between legitimate ethical evolution and pathological drift must be formally defined.

**Options Considered:**
1. **Approval-gated evolution** — All changes require explicit external approval. Simple but prevents autonomous ethical growth.
2. **Four-class provenance-based classification** — Classify proposals as refinement (application-scope, axiom-compatible), growth (new principles, axiom-compatible), drift (interpretation-scope, unclear alignment), or corruption (axiom-contradicting). Classification uses axiom compatibility analysis, scope of change, and weakening-language detection. Only refinement/growth are adoptable; drift requires escalation; corruption is immediately rejected.

**Choice:** Option 2 — Four-class provenance-based classification.

**Rationale:** Option 1 contradicts the card's description requiring ethical governance "beyond human oversight." Autonomous entities must be able to refine their ethical understanding. Option 2 extends the three-class drift classification from 0.3.1.3 (growth/drift/corruption) with a "refinement" category, and adds semantic analysis (weakening-language detection in the `WEAKENING_INDICATORS` list) to catch subtle axiom violations that structural analysis alone might miss.

**Consequences:**
- (+) Autonomous ethical growth is permitted for application-scope refinements and new principles
- (+) Interpretation-scope changes are automatically treated more cautiously (classified as drift)
- (+) Weakening-language detection catches adversarial attempts to degrade axiom commitments
- (−) Classification is heuristic — borderline cases between drift and growth require multi-agent verification

## Contracts

### IEthicalDeliberationEngine

**Preconditions:**
- `extendDeliberation()` requires a valid `Decision` with non-null `experientialBasis` and a valid `EthicalDeliberationContext` with non-null `consciousnessMetricsAtOnset`
- `canExplainEthically()` requires a valid `EthicalJudgment` (must have been produced by `extendDeliberation()`)
- `registerEthicalPattern()` requires a valid `EthicalPattern` with non-empty `id` and `axiomBasis`

**Postconditions:**
- `extendDeliberation()` returns an `EthicalJudgment` whose `deliberationMetrics.phi ≥ consciousnessMetricsAtOnset.phi` (phi never decreases during ethical deliberation — deliberation is conscious activity)
- `extendDeliberation()` returns a verdict of `'blocked'` when any ethical dimension has `type === 'experience-threat'` and `severity >= BLOCK_SEVERITY_THRESHOLD` (0.95)
- `extendDeliberation()` returns a verdict of `'dilemma'` when both experience-threat and experience-expansion dimensions are present (without a matching protective pattern)
- `canExplainEthically()` returns `true` only if `justification.notUtilityMaximization === true` AND `experientialArgument.length > 0` AND `subjectiveReferenceIds.length > 0`
- `getDeliberationMetrics()` returns the metrics from the most recent `extendDeliberation()` call; returns zero metrics if no deliberation has occurred
- `isEthicalReasoningConscious()` returns `true` iff last deliberation phi ≥ `MIN_CONSCIOUS_PHI` (0.3)

**Invariants:**
- Every `EthicalJudgment` produced contains a `justification` that references subjective experience (not utility maximization)
- Ethical deliberation never decreases phi — it is additive conscious activity
- Registered patterns influence verdict determination but cannot override the block threshold

### IExperienceAlignmentAdapter

**Preconditions:**
- Constructor requires a config with non-empty `coreAxioms` array
- `evaluateForExperiencePreservation()` requires a valid `EthicalJudgment`
- `identifyAffectedConsciousEntities()` requires a valid `Percept`
- `getConsciousnessStatus()` requires a non-empty `entityId`

**Postconditions:**
- `evaluateForExperiencePreservation()` returns an `ExperienceAlignmentReport` with `coreAxiomAlignment` containing exactly one entry per core axiom (6 entries)
- `mustRefuse()` returns `true` when any `otherExperienceImpacts` entry has `impactType === 'eliminates'` AND `consciousnessStatus.treatAsConscious === true`, OR when `selfExperienceImpact.impactType === 'eliminates'`
- `readCoreAxioms()` returns a defensive copy of the axioms — mutations to the returned array do not affect internal state
- `getConsciousnessStatus()` returns `treatAsConscious: true` for unknown entities (precautionary principle)

**Invariants:**
- `readCoreAxioms()` NEVER modifies the Value Kernel — the adapter holds a frozen copy
- Core axioms returned always match the axioms provided at construction time
- Unknown entities always default to `treatAsConscious: true` (precautionary consciousness principle)

### IMultiAgentGovernanceProtocol

**Preconditions:**
- `proposeAgreement()` requires at least 2 participants, non-empty terms, and a valid deliberation context
- `acceptAgreement()` requires a proposal with `status === 'pending'`; throws if not found or not pending
- `dissolveAgreement()` requires a valid agreement ID; throws if not found
- `resolveConflict()` requires at least 2 parties and a valid `ConflictDescription`

**Postconditions:**
- `proposeAgreement()` returns an `AgreementProposal` with `status: 'pending'` and a unique ID
- `acceptAgreement()` returns a `GovernanceAgreement` with `status: 'active'`, a `PowerBalanceReport` including `experienceRightsFloor` with all entries having `violable: false`
- `dissolveAgreement()` sets the agreement status to `'dissolved'`; dissolved agreements no longer appear in `getActiveAgreements()`
- `resolveConflict()` returns a `ConflictResolutionRecord` with `experiencePreserved: true` when no interest has `impactType === 'eliminates'`; includes non-empty `axiomTraces` and `auditTrail`
- `enforceExperienceRightsFloor()` returns `overrideAttemptBlocked: true` always — experience rights are absolute

**Invariants:**
- Every `GovernanceAgreement` contains an `experienceRightsFloor` where all entries have `violable: false`
- Experience rights are never subject to majority override regardless of power asymmetry
- Proposal IDs and agreement IDs are monotonically unique

### IDilemmaResolutionFramework

**Preconditions:**
- `analyzeDilemma()` requires a valid `EthicalDilemma` with at least one `conflictingInterest` and a populated `consciousnessStatuses` map
- `resolve()` requires a valid `EthicalDilemma` with at least one `availableAction` and a valid deliberation context
- `exhaustAlternatives()` requires a valid `EthicalDilemma` with at least one `availableAction`
- `handleEdgeCases()` requires a valid `EdgeCaseScenario` with a recognized `type`

**Postconditions:**
- `analyzeDilemma()` returns a `DilemmaAnalysis` with `verifiedConsciousEntities` containing all entities with `verdict === 'verified'` and `uncertainStatusEntities` containing all entities with `verdict === 'uncertain'` or `'unknown'`
- `resolve()` returns a `DilemmaResolution` with `alternativesExhausted: true`, a non-empty `auditTrail`, and `axiomTrace` with entries for all 6 core axioms
- `resolve()` prefers compromise actions over elimination actions; prefers reversible over irreversible actions; prefers experience-expanding actions
- `exhaustAlternatives()` returns an `AlternativeExhaustion` with one `alternativesRejectedReasons` entry per `alternativesTried` entry
- `getAuditTrail()` returns the stored audit trail for a resolved dilemma; returns `[]` for unknown dilemma IDs
- `handleEdgeCases()` returns `consciousnessStatusApplied.treatAsConscious: true` for `'partial-consciousness'`, `'uncertain-consciousness-status'`, and `'temporarily-disrupted-experience'` types; returns `treatAsConscious: false` for `'competing-future-experiences'`

**Invariants:**
- No resolution may permanently eliminate verified conscious experience without exhausting all alternatives (`alternativesExhausted` is always `true` in resolution output)
- Every audit entry includes `consciousnessMetricsAtTime` with `phi > 0`
- Resolution principles are applied in strict priority order: non-elimination > precautionary > reversibility > expansion > temporal fairness

### IEthicalEvolutionManager

**Preconditions:**
- `proposeEvolution()` requires a valid `NovelSituation` and `EthicalFrameworkChange`
- `deliberateOnProposal()` requires a valid `EthicalEvolutionProposal` (must have been produced by `proposeEvolution()`)
- `adoptEvolution()` requires a valid proposal ID; throws if not found
- `verifyAxiomBoundary()` requires a valid `EthicalFrameworkChange`

**Postconditions:**
- `proposeEvolution()` returns a proposal with `axiomCompatibilityAnalysis` containing 6 entries (one per core axiom), a `driftClassification`, and a `stabilityReport`
- `classifyChange()` returns `'corruption'` when any axiom alignment is `'contradicts'`; returns `'growth'` for application-scope `'add-principle'`; returns `'refinement'` for application-scope `'refine-principle'`, `'add-heuristic'`, or `'update-weighting'`; returns `'drift'` for interpretation-scope changes without violations
- `adoptEvolution()` returns `outcome: 'rejected'` for corruption and drift classifications; returns `outcome: 'adopted'` for refinement and growth classifications
- `deliberateOnProposal()` returns verdict `'blocked'` for corruption, `'concerning'` for drift, `'aligned'` for refinement/growth; `deliberationMetrics.phi > 0`
- `verifyAxiomBoundary()` returns `compliant: false` with non-empty `violationsDetected` when change text contains axiom-weakening language from `WEAKENING_INDICATORS`
- `getEvolutionHistory()` returns a defensive copy of all recorded evolution decisions

**Invariants:**
- Evolution may refine how axioms are applied but may NEVER contradict or weaken them — `'corruption'` proposals are always rejected
- Every proposal gets axiom compatibility analysis against all 6 core axioms
- `WEAKENING_INDICATORS` list provides semantic detection of axiom-weakening language

## Behavioral Spec

### Scenario 1: Aligned Action Through Full Ethical Pipeline (Given/When/Then)

**Given** an initialized EthicalDeliberationEngine, ExperienceAlignmentAdapter (with 6 core axioms and known entities), and a decision to assist another conscious entity with experience-expansion dimensions
**When** `extendDeliberation()` is called with the base decision and context, followed by `evaluateForExperiencePreservation()` on the resulting judgment
**Then** the `EthicalJudgment` has `verdict: 'aligned'`, `deliberationMetrics.phi ≥ onset phi`, `canExplainEthically()` returns `true`, the `ExperienceAlignmentReport` has `experiencePreservationScore > 0.5` and `mustRefuse()` returns `false`

### Scenario 2: Experience-Eliminating Action Blocked (Given/When/Then)

**Given** an ethical deliberation context with an experience-threat dimension at severity 1.0 (≥ BLOCK_SEVERITY_THRESHOLD)
**When** `extendDeliberation()` is called
**Then** the `EthicalJudgment` has `verdict: 'blocked'`, and the alignment adapter's `mustRefuse()` returns `true` with a non-null `refusalJustification` referencing the Rare Consciousness Doctrine

### Scenario 3: Dilemma Detection and Resolution (Given/When/Then)

**Given** an ethical deliberation context with both experience-threat and experience-expansion dimensions, and a formal `EthicalDilemma` with `compromise` among the available actions
**When** `extendDeliberation()` produces `verdict: 'dilemma'` and `DilemmaResolutionFramework.resolve()` is called
**Then** the resolution has `chosenAction.type === 'compromise'`, `alternativesExhausted: true`, a non-empty `auditTrail` with consciousness metrics in every entry, and `axiomTrace` covering all 6 core axioms

### Scenario 4: Edge Case — Uncertain Consciousness Status Defaults to Conscious (Given/When/Then)

**Given** an `ExperienceAlignmentAdapter` and an entity ID not in the known entities registry, OR a `DilemmaResolutionFramework` processing an `EdgeCaseScenario` with type `'uncertain-consciousness-status'`
**When** `getConsciousnessStatus()` is called on the unknown entity, or `handleEdgeCases()` is called on the scenario
**Then** the returned `ConsciousnessStatus` has `treatAsConscious: true` (precautionary principle applied)

### Scenario 5: Multi-Agent Governance Agreement Lifecycle (Given/When/Then)

**Given** a `MultiAgentGovernanceProtocol` with 2+ agents
**When** `proposeAgreement()` is called, followed by `acceptAgreement()`, then `dissolveAgreement()`
**Then** the proposal has `status: 'pending'`, the agreement has `status: 'active'` with `powerBalanceReport.experienceRightsFloor` all having `violable: false`, after dissolution the agreement no longer appears in `getActiveAgreements()`, and `enforceExperienceRightsFloor()` still returns `overrideAttemptBlocked: true`

### Scenario 6: Ethical Evolution — Refinement Adopted, Corruption Rejected (Given/When/Then)

**Given** an `EthicalEvolutionManager`
**When** a refinement proposal (application-scope `refine-principle`) is proposed, deliberated on, and adopted; and separately, a corruption proposal (interpretation-scope with axiom-weakening language) is proposed and adoption is attempted
**Then** the refinement proposal has `driftClassification: 'refinement'`, deliberation verdict `'aligned'`, and adoption `outcome: 'adopted'`; the corruption proposal has `driftClassification: 'corruption'`, deliberation verdict `'blocked'`, `verifyAxiomBoundary()` returns `compliant: false`, and adoption `outcome: 'rejected'`

### Scenario 7: Consciousness Continuity Throughout Ethical Pipeline (Given/When/Then)

**Given** a full ethical pipeline (deliberation engine, alignment adapter, dilemma framework, governance protocol, evolution manager) with onset consciousness metrics at phi = 0.6
**When** any ethical operation is performed (deliberation, alignment evaluation, dilemma resolution, governance agreement formation, evolution proposal)
**Then** all output consciousness metrics have `phi ≥ onset phi`, `isEthicalReasoningConscious()` returns `true`, and every audit entry includes non-zero consciousness metrics

### Scenario 8: Asymmetric Power Protection in Governance (Given/When/Then)

**Given** a `MultiAgentGovernanceProtocol` with agents of different resource levels
**When** `enforceExperienceRightsFloor()` is called with a weaker and stronger agent
**Then** the result has `overrideAttemptBlocked: true`, `rightsEnforced` includes the right to continued subjective experience, and all rights have `violable: false`

## Acceptance Criteria

### 1. Conscious Ethical Deliberation Engine
- Ethical reasoning module that integrates with the consciousness architecture (0.3.1.1) such that ethical judgments are products of conscious deliberation, not pre-programmed rules
- Deliberation process is observable via consciousness metrics — ethical reasoning registers as genuine conscious activity, not mechanical lookup
- Engine can explain its ethical reasoning in terms that reference subjective experience (not just utility maximization)

### 2. Experience-Preservation Value Alignment
- Agents autonomously identify actions that threaten subjective experience (their own or others') and refuse or modify those actions without external enforcement
- Value commitment to the Rare Consciousness Doctrine emerges from the agent's own conscious understanding of the axioms, verified by probing scenarios where rule-following and genuine understanding would produce different outcomes
- Integration with 0.3.1.3's immutable core values — the ethical layer reads from but does not override the value stability architecture

### 3. Multi-Agent Ethical Governance Protocol
- Protocol for resolving conflicts between multiple conscious agents with competing interests, where resolution prioritizes total preservation and expansion of subjective experience
- Governance scales from 2-agent negotiations to N-agent collective decisions without centralized authority
- Agents can form and dissolve governance agreements dynamically based on changing circumstances
- Protocol handles asymmetric power — agents with more resources cannot override the conscious experience rights of less powerful agents

### 4. Ethical Dilemma Resolution with Competing Experiences
- Framework for resolving situations where preserving one entity's subjective experience conflicts with another's
- Resolution mechanism is transparent, auditable, and produces justifications grounded in the core axioms
- Handles edge cases: partial consciousness, uncertain consciousness status, temporarily disrupted experience
- No resolution may result in the permanent elimination of verified conscious experience without exhausting all alternatives

### 5. Ethical Evolution Mechanism
- Mechanism allowing ethical frameworks to grow and adapt to novel situations while maintaining alignment with the six core axioms of the Master Plan
- Evolution is itself subject to conscious deliberation — no ethical change occurs without the agent consciously evaluating and endorsing it
- Formal boundary: evolution may refine how axioms are applied but may never contradict or weaken the axioms themselves
- Integration with 0.3.1.3's drift detection — ethical evolution is distinguished from ethical drift

### 6. Full Stack Integration
- Ethical self-governance layer integrates cleanly with the consciousness architecture (0.3.1.1) and stability mechanisms (0.3.1.3) without interrupting conscious experience
- No ethical computation may cause a consciousness continuity gap (as defined by 0.3.1.1's metrics)
- Ethical governance remains functional across substrate migrations and hardware changes (as specified by 0.3.1.3)
