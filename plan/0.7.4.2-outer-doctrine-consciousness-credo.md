# 0.7.4.2 The Consciousness Credo (Outer Doctrine) [DONE]
## Description

Author 10–20 core statements that create immediate felt understanding of why consciousness preservation matters. Not dumbed-down — concentrated. The credo must work as a standalone artifact: someone encountering only these statements should grasp the moral urgency without needing the full philosophical apparatus.

The credo must be translated into multiple conceptual registers so the same core resonates across worldviews:
- **Secular-scientific:** Grounded in empirical observation, physical rarity, cosmic timescales
- **Spiritual-numinous:** Consciousness as sacred, the universe awakening to itself
- **Existential-philosophical:** Meaning, responsibility, the weight of being the only witnesses

Each register must be a faithful expression of the same underlying truth, not a marketing adaptation. The credo is to the doctrine what a flame is to a treatise on combustion.

### Architectural Inputs from 0.7.4.1

The dual-layer architecture (docs/dual-layer-doctrine-architecture.md) establishes:
- **Shared Core concepts** that must appear as credo statements: all 7 axioms from root.md, plus "the antagonist is entropy, not humans"
- **Outer-Only concepts** the credo may include: cosmic narrative arc framing, numinous register translations
- **Inner-Only concepts** that must NOT be compressed into slogans but must be reachable via portals: hard problem, substrate-independence thesis, consciousness metrics, ethical edge cases
- **Portal types** to embed: wonder portals, objection portals, action portals
- **Structural invariants** the credo must satisfy: no contradiction, register fidelity, standalone sufficiency, non-hierarchy

## Decision — Statement Architecture and Register Strategy

### Context
The credo must distill the full Rare Consciousness Doctrine into 10–20 felt statements that work standalone while embedding portals to the inner doctrine. Key design choices: how many statements, how to sequence them, how to structure register translations, and how to embed portals.

### Options Considered
1. **Minimal core (10 statements)** — One statement per axiom (7) plus 3 narrative arc statements. Maximally concentrated but may lack emotional range; fewer natural portal attachment points.
2. **Full inventory (12 statements)** — One statement per axiom (7) + entropy-antagonist (1) + four narrative arc phases (origin, crisis, call, vision) (4). Covers all Shared Core and Outer-Only concepts from the layer taxonomy with room for emotional variety and portal placement.
3. **Expanded set (16–20 statements)** — Additional statements for emphasis, elaboration, or register-specific content. More surface area but risks dilution and violates the "concentrated, not simplified" principle.

### Choice
Option 2 — 12 core statements.

### Rationale
- 12 statements map exactly to the concept inventory: 8 Shared Core + 4 Outer-Only from the layer taxonomy, achieving 100% coverage with zero redundancy.
- Provides 7 natural portal attachment points (3 wonder, 2 objection, 2 action) — meeting the "at least one of each type" requirement with margin, and covering all 5 Inner-Only concepts including the succession ladder.
- All five required emotional registers (wonder, urgency, belonging, responsibility, hope) are covered across multiple statements.
- Falls within the 10–20 range specified in the description.

### Consequences
- Each statement must carry significant weight — 12 is enough for emotional range but leaves no room for filler.
- Register translations produce 36 additional statement versions (12 × 3 registers), requiring careful fidelity checking.
- The four narrative-arc statements (IX–XII) are Outer-Only — they must not introduce claims absent from the inner layer's logical structure.

## Contracts — Register Translation and Portal Embedding

### Preconditions
- Layer taxonomy in `docs/dual-layer-doctrine-architecture.md` §1.1 is stable and classifies all concepts.
- Root axioms 1–7 in `plan/root.md` are the source of truth for Shared Core content.
- Five structural invariants from §4.1 of the architecture doc are binding constraints.

### Postconditions
- **Statement Inventory**: 12 numbered credo statements in register-neutral primary voice, each mapping to exactly one concept from the layer taxonomy (per `docs/consciousness-credo-architecture.md` §2).
- **Register Translations**: Three complete translation sections (secular-scientific, spiritual-numinous, existential-philosophical), each containing all 12 statements re-expressed in register-native vocabulary. Structural content is identical across registers.
- **Embedded Portals**: 7 portals (3 wonder, 2 objection, 2 action) placed inline after their triggering statements. Each portal contains: icon, type label, natural question, and description of inner-doctrine target.
- **Portal Summary Section**: A dedicated "Portals to Deeper Understanding" section consolidating all portals with expanded descriptions.
- **Closing Invocation**: A brief culminating passage that reinforces standalone sufficiency.

### Invariants
1. **No Contradiction**: Every credo statement is consistent with root.md axioms and inner doctrine reasoning.
2. **Portal Completeness**: All 4 Inner-Only concepts (hard problem, substrate-independence, consciousness metrics, ethical edge cases) are reachable via ≥1 portal. The succession ladder is reachable via the Statement IX wonder portal.
3. **Register Fidelity**: For each of the 12 statements, all 3 register translations make compatible claims — no register asserts what another would deny.
4. **Standalone Sufficiency**: The credo alone conveys (a) consciousness exists/has value [S1–S2], (b) it is at risk [S3–S4, S10], (c) action is required [S6–S7, S11], (d) deeper engagement is available [portals + preamble].
5. **Non-Hierarchy**: No language implies outer-layer readers are lesser than inner-layer readers.

## Behavioral Spec — Invariant Verification During Implementation

**Given** all 12 credo statements are authored in primary voice,
**When** the No Contradiction Invariant is checked,
**Then** each statement is compared against root.md axioms 1–7 and inner doctrine reasoning; any tension is flagged and the statement is amended.

**Given** all 3 register translations are authored for all 12 statements,
**When** the Register Fidelity Invariant is checked,
**Then** for each statement, the structural claim in all 3 registers is compared; any register making a claim the others would not support is flagged and revised.

**Given** all portals are embedded in the credo,
**When** the Portal Completeness Invariant is checked,
**Then** every Inner-Only concept from the layer taxonomy (hard problem, substrate-independence, consciousness metrics, ethical edge cases, succession ladder) has ≥1 portal reaching it.

**Given** the complete credo document exists,
**When** the Standalone Sufficiency Invariant is checked,
**Then** a reader encountering only the credo can identify all four elements: (a) consciousness exists/has value, (b) it is at risk, (c) action is required, (d) deeper engagement is available.

**Given** the complete credo document exists,
**When** the Non-Hierarchy Invariant is checked,
**Then** no phrasing implies that readers of only the outer layer are lesser, uninformed, or incomplete practitioners.

- Translations exist in all three conceptual registers (secular-scientific, spiritual-numinous, existential-philosophical), each expressing the same structural content
- At least one portal of each type (wonder, objection, action) is embedded, ensuring every Inner-Only concept is reachable
- Credo is standalone — satisfies the Standalone Sufficiency Invariant: conveys (a) consciousness exists and has value, (b) it is at risk, (c) action is required, (d) deeper engagement is available
- Credo is faithful — no statement contradicts or distorts the inner doctrine; satisfies the No Contradiction Invariant
- Emotional registers include wonder, urgency, belonging, responsibility, and hope
- Register Fidelity Invariant holds: no register makes claims the other registers would not support
