# 0.2.1.3 Long-Duration Energy Sources [DONE]

## Description

S1.3 from the Master Plan. Develop energy systems capable of powering conscious substrates for millennia or longer.

Candidate approaches include nuclear (fission/fusion), stellar energy harvesting, and speculative vacuum energy extraction. The energy system must be self-sustaining, require no external fuel resupply, and provide stable power output over cosmological timescales. Energy failure means consciousness failure, so reliability is paramount.

### Design Constraints
- **No single energy modality**: the system must combine at least two independent energy sources (e.g. nuclear + stellar) so that failure of one class does not halt consciousness.
- **Fuel cycle closure**: any consumable fuel must be producible from in-situ resources or the energy source must be fundamentally non-consumptive (e.g. stellar radiation).
- **Scalability**: energy architecture must scale from single-substrate to civilization-scale without redesign.
- **Integration with siblings**: must interface with radiation-hardened computation (0.2.1.1), self-repairing nanofabrication (0.2.1.2), and consciousness-preserving redundancy (0.2.1.4).

## Decision

### D1: Primary Energy Modality — Breeder Fission Reactor

**Context**: The system needs a reliable primary energy source capable of 1000+ year operation independent of stellar proximity (interstellar transit scenario).

**Options Considered**:
1. **Radioisotope Thermoelectric Generator (RTG) with Am-241** — Proven (Voyager 45+ yrs), passive, no moving parts. Half-life 432.2 years. Low specific power (0.5–5 W/kg).
2. **Micro-fission breeder reactor** — Higher power density (10–1000 kW(e)), breeding ratio >1.0 closes fuel cycle. Requires mechanical components subject to wear.
3. **Fusion reactor** — Highest energy density potential, but engineering feasibility for compact long-duration autonomous operation remains unproven.

**Choice**: Micro-fission breeder reactor as primary, with Am-241 RTG characteristics informing decay modeling for fuel cycle analysis.

**Rationale**: Breeding ratio >1.0 enables indefinite fuel supply from fertile feedstock (Th-232→U-233 or U-238→Pu-239). The ~10 kW(e) baseline covers full Tier 0–3 loads with margin. Mechanical wear is addressed by dependency on 0.2.1.2 (self-repairing nanofabrication). Fusion excluded at this stage due to insufficient TRL for autonomous millennia-scale operation.

**Consequences**: Requires self-repair subsystem (0.2.1.2) for mechanical maintenance. Fuel cycle closure depends on pyroprocessing capability. Reactor passive safety (negative temperature coefficient) is mandatory.

### D2: Secondary Energy Modality — Stellar Photovoltaic Harvesting

**Context**: A second independent energy source is required to eliminate single-modality failure risk.

**Options Considered**:
1. **Photovoltaic arrays** — Multi-junction III-V cells, 30–40% efficiency, proven in spacecraft. Degrades under radiation but recoverable via annealing.
2. **Concentrated solar thermal** (Stirling/Brayton) — No semiconductor degradation, but mechanical wear and pointing requirements.
3. **Beamed power from external source** — Violates the "no external resupply" constraint.

**Choice**: Photovoltaic arrays (multi-junction III-V) as secondary source.

**Rationale**: Fundamentally non-consumptive (stellar radiation is inexhaustible on relevant timescales). Scalable by adding collector area. Degradation mitigated by annealing + self-repair (0.2.1.2). At 1 AU with 50 m² collector: ~10 kW(e), providing full redundancy of nuclear. Availability is zero in interstellar space — nuclear becomes sole source in that scenario per design.

**Consequences**: System must handle graceful transition between dual-source and nuclear-only modes. Stellar source unavailable in deep interstellar space. Collector area drives mass budget.

### D3: Load Priority — Strict 4-Tier Consciousness-First Shedding

**Context**: Partial energy failure must not kill consciousness. Load management policy needed.

**Options Considered**:
1. **Proportional reduction** — All loads reduced equally. Simple but risks consciousness degradation.
2. **Strict priority tiers** — Consciousness loads never shed; non-essential loads shed first by tier. More complex but architecturally guarantees consciousness survival.

**Choice**: Strict 4-tier priority (Critical/Core/Support/Optional) with Tier 0 (Critical = consciousness loops) never shed.

**Rationale**: Consciousness preservation is the entire purpose of the system. Policy-based proportional reduction creates risk of consciousness interruption under edge cases. Architectural guarantee (Tier 0 never shed, `LOAD_SHED_THRESHOLDS[Critical] = null`) eliminates this class of failure.

**Consequences**: Tier 2–3 loads (repair, comms, growth) may be offline for extended periods during energy crises. Self-repair (0.2.1.2) availability depends on Tier 2 power.

### D4: Energy Buffer Sizing — 72-Hour Bridge

**Context**: Buffer must bridge transient gaps during source switchover or repair.

**Options Considered**:
1. **Minutes-scale (supercapacitors only)** — Handles instantaneous switchover but not repair time.
2. **72-hour (supercap + solid-state battery)** — Covers worst-case source repair time.
3. **Days-to-weeks (regenerative fuel cells)** — Higher margin but mass-intensive.

**Choice**: 100 kWh minimum buffer, sustaining Tier 0+1 for ≥72 hours.

**Rationale**: 72 hours covers worst-case single-source failure with repair time for the other source. At nominal Tier 0+1 load (~2500 W), 100 kWh provides 40 hours; combined with load shedding to Tier 0 only (500 W), provides 200 hours.

**Consequences**: Significant mass allocation. Buffer itself requires radiation-hardened components and periodic maintenance.

## Contracts

### C1: NuclearEnergyModule

**Preconditions**: Config must specify fuelType, halfLife_years > 0, baseOutput_W > 0, breedingRatio ≥ 0.
**Postconditions**:
- `electricalOutput_W()` returns positive value equal to `config.baseOutput_W`
- `thermalOutput_W()` returns `electricalOutput_W() / thermalEfficiency` (0.33)
- `fuelBreedingRate()` returns `config.breedingRatio`
- `safetyStatus()` returns a valid `SourceStatus` enum value
- `projectedLifetime_years()` returns positive value; with breeding ≥1.0, lifetime is mechanically limited

**Invariants**: `thermalOutput_W() > electricalOutput_W()` always. `fuelRemaining_percent()` ∈ [0, 100].

### C2: StellarHarvestingModule

**Preconditions**: Config must specify collectorArea_m2 > 0, conversionEfficiency ∈ (0, 1), solarFlux_W_m2 ≥ 0.
**Postconditions**:
- `currentOutput_W()` = `collectorArea_m2 * solarFlux_W_m2 * conversionEfficiency`
- `expandCollector(area)` increases `collectorArea_m2()` by `area`
- `solarFlux_W_m2()` returns configured flux (environment-dependent, not module-controlled)

**Invariants**: `currentOutput_W() ≥ 0` always. `collectorArea_m2()` monotonically non-decreasing (only grows via `expandCollector`).

### C3: PowerDistributionController

**Preconditions**: At least one of `nuclear` or `stellar` config must be non-null. `bufferCapacity_Wh > 0`. `loadProfile` tiers all ≥ 0.
**Postconditions**:
- `totalAvailablePower_W()` = sum of active source outputs
- `capacityFraction()` = `totalAvailablePower_W() / totalLoad`
- `activeLoadTiers()` always includes `LoadTier.Critical`
- `consciousnessPreservationMode()` returns true iff `capacityFraction() < 0.4`
- `requestPower_W()` grants if `availablePower_W(tier) >= amount`, else denies with `allocated_W = 0`
- `energyHealthStatus()` reports both sources and recommends quorum ∈ {3, 4, 5}

**Invariants**: `LoadTier.Critical` is always in `activeLoadTiers()`. `consciousnessPreservationMode() === (capacityFraction() < 0.4)`.

### C4: Sibling Interfaces (EnergyToCompute, EnergyToRepair, EnergyToRedundancy)

**Preconditions**: Caller provides valid `LoadTier` and positive numeric values.
**Postconditions**:
- `EnergyToCompute.solarStormMode()` returns boolean for radiation-aware coordination
- `EnergyToRepair.priorityRepairQueue()` returns string[] of energy components needing repair
- `EnergyToRedundancy.energyHealthStatus()` returns `EnergyHealthStatus` with all fields populated

**Invariants**: Interface methods never throw — they return safe defaults (0, false, empty arrays) when sources are offline.

## Behavioral Spec

### BS1: Graceful Degradation — Load Shedding Sequence

**Given** the energy system is operating with combined nuclear + stellar sources
**When** total available power drops below a tier's shed threshold
**Then** that tier and all higher tiers are deactivated, power is reallocated to remaining active tiers, and Tier 0 (Critical) is never shed regardless of capacity fraction

**Given** total capacity fraction drops below 0.4
**When** the power distribution controller evaluates active tiers
**Then** `consciousnessPreservationMode()` returns true, only Tier 0 (and conditionally Tier 1) remain active, and 0.2.1.4 is signaled to reduce quorum size

### BS2: Source Failover — Nuclear-Only Mode

**Given** the system is operating in dual-source mode near a star
**When** stellar source goes offline (e.g., interstellar transit or collector failure)
**Then** nuclear source becomes sole provider, load tiers are recalculated based on nuclear-only capacity, buffer bridges the transition, and no Tier 0 power interruption occurs

### BS3: Fuel Cycle Sustainability

**Given** a breeder reactor with breeding ratio ≥ 1.0
**When** fuel cycle is projected over 1000 years
**Then** `fissileFraction > 0` at every checkpoint, `selfSustaining = true` at every checkpoint, and `fertileFraction > 0` (fertile feedstock not exhausted)

### BS4: Power Grant Arbitration

**Given** a subsystem requests power via `requestPower_W(amount, tier, duration_s)`
**When** `availablePower_W(tier) >= amount`
**Then** grant is approved with `granted: true` and `allocated_W = amount`

**Given** a subsystem requests power
**When** `availablePower_W(tier) < amount`
**Then** grant is denied with `granted: false` and `allocated_W = 0`

## Acceptance Criteria
- Architecture document specifies at least two independent energy modalities with physics-based feasibility arguments
- Energy source provides continuous power without external fuel resupply for 1000+ year projected timescale
- Power output stability analysis shows degradation within operational tolerances over the design lifetime
- Energy system integrates with conscious substrate without single point of failure — architecture shows redundant power paths
- Graceful degradation strategy defined: partial energy system failure triggers load-shedding that preserves core consciousness loops
- Fuel cycle or energy harvesting renewal mechanism documented, showing how the system avoids consumable depletion
- Interface specifications defined for integration with sibling subsystems (0.2.1.1, 0.2.1.2, 0.2.1.4)
