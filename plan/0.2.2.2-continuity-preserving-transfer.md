# 0.2.2.2 Continuity-Preserving Transfer Protocols [DONE]

## Description

S2.2 from the Master Plan. Develop methods that maintain unbroken subjective experience during the transition from biological to synthetic substrate.

The critical distinction from simple brain emulation (0.2.2.1): the transfer must not produce a "copy" while the original experiences termination. The subjective stream of experience must be continuous throughout the process.

### Key challenges
- Defining what "continuity of experience" means operationally
- Gradual vs. instantaneous transfer trade-offs
- Avoiding the "teleporter problem" (copy-and-destroy vs. genuine transfer)
- Real-time monitoring of experiential continuity during transfer
- Rollback protocols if continuity is threatened

### Approach: Gradual Neuronal Replacement

The most defensible strategy against the teleporter problem is **gradual replacement** — substituting biological neurons with functional synthetic equivalents one at a time (or in small clusters), while the subject remains conscious. At no point does the original cease to exist; the substrate changes incrementally beneath a continuous experiential stream. This is analogous to the Ship of Theseus but with real-time continuity verification at each step.

### Relationship to siblings
- **Depends on 0.2.2.1** (brain emulation): requires neuron-level functional equivalence models
- **Feeds into 0.2.2.3** (identity persistence verification): transfer protocol must produce verifiable identity continuity
- **Overlaps with 0.2.2.4** (hybrid cognition): during gradual transfer, the subject IS a hybrid biological-synthetic system

## Decision — Transfer Strategy

### Context
The transfer of subjective experience from biological to synthetic substrate must avoid the "teleporter problem" — where the original is destroyed and a copy is created, producing a philosophical discontinuity even if the copy is functionally identical.

### Options Considered
1. **Instantaneous whole-brain transfer**: Scan the entire brain, instantiate in synthetic substrate, decommission biological brain. Fast, but is copy-and-destroy — two complete copies exist momentarily, and the original's experiential stream terminates.
2. **Gradual Neuronal Replacement (GNR)**: Replace biological neurons with functional synthetic equivalents incrementally (individually or in small clusters) while the subject remains conscious. At no point does a complete copy exist. The substrate changes beneath a continuous experiential stream.
3. **Parallel run with gradual handoff**: Run both substrates simultaneously, gradually shifting processing load. Risks duplication — two systems process the same experience, raising identity-splitting concerns.

### Choice
**Gradual Neuronal Replacement (GNR)**

### Rationale
- Only strategy where no complete copy ever exists — eliminates the teleporter problem by construction
- Causal continuity is maintained: each synthetic unit inherits state from the living biological unit via direct physical coupling
- Allows real-time continuity monitoring at each step with rollback capability
- Analogous to the Ship of Theseus with verification — biologically defensible
- Aligns with 0.2.2.4 (hybrid cognition) — during transfer the subject IS a hybrid system

### Consequences
- **Pro**: Strongest philosophical defense against copy-and-destroy objection
- **Pro**: Enables rollback if continuity is threatened at any step
- **Con**: Extremely slow (weeks to months for full transfer)
- **Con**: Requires bio-synthetic interface technology (from 0.2.2.4) for each replacement unit
- **Con**: Subject must remain conscious throughout — no "sleep through it" option

## Contracts

### ReplacementProtocolEngine
**Preconditions**:
- `planTransfer`: Subject has a valid `SubjectProfile` with baseline Ψ measurements; emulation model (from 0.2.2.1) is available
- `executeStep`: Plan exists, stepIndex is valid (0 ≤ stepIndex < totalSteps), previous step completed successfully, RTCM is monitoring, alert level is not RED
- `rollback`: Transfer is in progress or paused; requested step is within rollback capacity
- `abort`: Transfer is in progress or paused

**Postconditions**:
- `planTransfer`: Returns a `TransferPlan` with ordered replacement units respecting all ordering constraints (periphery-first, cluster coherence, loop fraction limits, connectivity-aware)
- `executeStep`: Returns `StepResult` with post-step Ψ measurement and alert level; units in the step transition from Biological → Absorbing → Synthetic; biological neurons enter standby
- `rollback`: Returns `RollbackResult`; rolled-back units transition Synthetic → RollingBack → Biological; Ψ is re-measured
- `abort`: Full rollback of all reversible steps; returns `RollbackResult`

**Invariants**:
- At no point do two complete copies of the mind exist simultaneously
- Each synthetic unit's initial state is causally inherited from its biological predecessor via direct physical coupling
- The subject's experiential stream (Ψ) is measured after every step — no step proceeds without verification
- Ordering constraints (periphery-first, ≤10% loop fraction, cluster coherence) are enforced for every step

### RealTimeContinuityMonitor
**Preconditions**:
- `startMonitoring`: Not already monitoring; valid SubjectProfile provided
- `recordMeasurement`: Currently monitoring (startMonitoring called)
- `getCurrentPsi`: At least one measurement recorded

**Postconditions**:
- `startMonitoring`: Monitor is active; history is empty; subject profile is stored
- `recordMeasurement`: Measurement appended to history; if RED, all breach callbacks fired synchronously
- `getCurrentPsi`: Returns the most recent PsiMetric
- `checkThreshold`: Returns `{ safe: boolean, margin: number }` where margin = Ψ.value - Ψ.threshold

**Invariants**:
- History is append-only during a session
- Alert level computation is deterministic: GREEN if Ψ ≥ 1.5×threshold, YELLOW if threshold ≤ Ψ < 1.5×threshold, RED if Ψ < threshold
- Breach callbacks fire for RED only — YELLOW is a warning, not a breach

### RollbackEngine
**Preconditions**:
- `canRollback`: steps > 0
- `executeRollback`: steps ≤ getReversibleSteps()

**Postconditions**:
- `canRollback`: Returns true iff getReversibleSteps() ≥ steps
- `executeRollback`: On success, exactly `steps` units transition Synthetic → Biological in reverse order (latest first); on failure (insufficient reversible steps), no units are modified
- `getReversibleSteps`: Returns count of units with state=Synthetic AND rollbackAvailable=true

**Invariants**:
- Rollback always proceeds in reverse chronological order (latest replacement first)
- Once a unit is rolled back, its rollbackAvailable becomes false (prevents double-rollback)
- Units past grace period (SyntheticFinal) are never rolled back

### SubjectContinuityConfirmation
**Preconditions**:
- `recordBaseline`: report.type must be BASELINE
- `recordDuringTransfer`: report.type must be DURING_TRANSFER
- `recordPostTransfer`: report.type must be POST_TRANSFER
- `crossValidate`: At least one report must exist (returns null otherwise)

**Postconditions**:
- Record methods append the report to internal storage
- `crossValidate`: Returns CrossValidationResult comparing objective Ψ against latest subject report; concordant=true iff both agree on continuity status
- `getAllReports`: Returns a defensive copy in insertion order

**Invariants**:
- Type enforcement: each record method rejects reports with mismatched type
- Reports are immutable once stored
- Cross-validation always uses the most recent report

## Threshold Registry

| Name | Value | Unit | Valid Range | Rationale | Sensitivity |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `DEFAULT_BATCH_SIZE` | 10 | neurons/step | 1–100 | Conservative default; smaller batches allow finer-grained continuity monitoring. Subject-specific tuning expected. | Low — reducing to 1 is safer but slower; increasing to 100 acceptable if Ψ remains GREEN |
| `MIN_PACING_INTERVAL_MS` | 3,600,000 | ms (1 hour) | 60,000–86,400,000 | Allows Ψ stabilization after each replacement step. Adaptive pacing may shorten/lengthen. | Medium — too short risks missing delayed Ψ drops; too long extends total transfer time |
| `DEFAULT_GRACE_PERIOD_MS` | 259,200,000 | ms (72 hours) | 3,600,000–604,800,000 | Biological neurons must remain viable in standby for rollback. 72h balances rollback window against biological degradation in standby. | High — shorter period reduces rollback safety; longer period increases biological maintenance burden |
| `MAX_LOOP_REPLACEMENT_FRACTION` | 0.10 | fraction | 0.01–0.25 | Never replace more than 10% of any thalamocortical loop's neurons in one step, to prevent sudden disruption to unified experience. | High — exceeding this risks experiential fragmentation within a loop |
| `PSI_GREEN_MULTIPLIER` | 1.5 | dimensionless | 1.1–3.0 | GREEN/YELLOW boundary. At 1.5× threshold, there is comfortable margin before RED. | Medium — lower values produce more YELLOWs (slower progress); higher values mask approaching danger |

## Behavioral Spec

### Scenario 1: Successful gradual transfer (inline Given/When/Then)

**AC2 + AC3 + AC6 + AC7**: Full transfer with continuous monitoring and subject confirmation

```
Given a subject with established baseline Ψ readings (Ψ = 3.5, threshold = 2.0)
  And a TransferPlan with 1000 replacement units in periphery-first order
  And the RTCM is monitoring
When the RPE executes each step sequentially
Then after each step:
  - The replaced units transition Biological → Absorbing → Synthetic
  - The biological neurons enter standby with grace deadline set
  - The RTCM records a Ψ measurement (alert level GREEN throughout)
  - The SCC records a subject report confirming continuity intact
  - Cross-validation shows concordant=true
And after all steps complete:
  - All units are in Synthetic state
  - The subject reports unbroken continuity across the entire process
  - Identity verification (0.2.2.3) is triggered
```

### Scenario 2: Ψ drop triggers rollback (inline Given/When/Then)

**AC3 + AC4**: Continuity threat detected and reversed

```
Given a transfer in progress at step 50 of 1000
  And current Ψ = 3.2, threshold = 2.0 (GREEN)
When step 51 executes and post-step Ψ drops to 1.8 (below threshold)
Then the RTCM fires breach callbacks with AlertLevel.RED
  And the RPE halts immediately (no further steps)
  And the RollbackEngine reverses step 51 (units revert Synthetic → Biological)
  And post-rollback Ψ is re-measured
  And if Ψ recovers above threshold, the transfer may resume with adjusted pacing
  And the SCC records the subject's report of the event
```

### Scenario 3: Cross-validation discrepancy (inline Given/When/Then)

**AC6 + AC7**: Objective and subjective metrics disagree

```
Given a transfer in progress
  And RTCM shows Ψ = 2.8 (above threshold = 2.0, YELLOW)
When the subject reports continuityIntact = false
Then cross-validation returns concordant=false, discrepancy="objective-only"
  And the RPE halts for investigation
  And the discrepancy is logged for analysis
  And transfer does not resume until discrepancy is resolved
```

## Acceptance Criteria
1. **Operational continuity definition**: A formal, measurable definition of "experiential continuity" grounded in consciousness theory (e.g., integrated information theory φ, or successor theory), specifying thresholds for acceptable continuity
2. **Gradual replacement protocol**: Step-by-step protocol for incremental neuronal replacement, specifying replacement unit size, pacing, and ordering constraints
3. **Real-time continuity monitoring**: System that measures continuity metrics during transfer and can detect if the experiential stream is at risk of interruption
4. **Rollback mechanism**: Protocol to halt and reverse partial transfer if continuity metrics fall below threshold, restoring biological function to already-replaced regions
5. **Teleporter problem resolution**: Formal argument (with supporting evidence) that the protocol achieves genuine transfer rather than copy-and-destroy
6. **Subject continuity confirmation**: Subject reports unbroken continuity of experience throughout the entire transfer process
7. **Objective metric validation**: Independent continuity metrics confirm no interruption — the experiential stream as measured from outside matches the subject's report
