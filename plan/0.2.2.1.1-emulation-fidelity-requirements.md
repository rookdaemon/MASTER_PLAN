# 0.2.2.1.1 Emulation Fidelity Requirements [DONE]

## Description

Determine the minimum resolution and fidelity at which a biological brain must be emulated to preserve subjective experience. This is the foundational question for all brain emulation work — the answer dictates the requirements for scanning (0.2.2.1.2), simulation (0.2.2.1.3), and validation (0.2.2.1.4).

Possible fidelity levels (from coarsest to finest):
- **Connectome-level**: neuron types, synaptic weights, network topology
- **Cellular-level**: individual neuron dynamics, dendritic computation, glial interactions
- **Molecular-level**: protein conformations, neurotransmitter dynamics, ion channel states
- **Quantum-level**: quantum coherence effects in microtubules or other structures

The required level depends entirely on what the F1 theory of consciousness (from 0.1.1) identifies as the physical substrate of subjective experience.

A specification document (`docs/emulation-fidelity-spec.md`) that downstream sibling cards consume as their input requirements.

## Decision: Minimum Emulation Fidelity Level (L_min)

### Context
ISMT defines consciousness via three conditions (IC, SM, GA) over a system's causal transition structure — the transition probability matrix T and interaction matrix W. An emulation preserves consciousness iff it reproduces the causal transition probabilities P(x_i(t+1) | x_j(t)) for all subsystem pairs participating in IC, SM, or GA. We must select the coarsest fidelity level that preserves these transitions.

### Options Considered

**L1: Connectome-level** — Neuron types, synaptic weights, network topology (~10^11 nodes, ~10^14 edges, static).
- Pros: Lowest computational cost; captures network structure.
- Cons: Static topology cannot implement SM (prediction error minimization is a dynamic process requiring temporal evolution); IC depends on mutual information between subsystem *dynamics*, not just topology; neuromodulatory state globally alters causal transitions and is invisible at this level.
- **Verdict: Insufficient.** Omits the temporal dynamics essential to all three ISMT conditions.

**L2: Cellular-level** — Individual neuron dynamics (Hodgkin-Huxley-class models), dendritic computation, glial interactions, neuromodulation (~10^11 neurons with multi-dimensional internal state).
- Pros: Captures the causal transitions underlying thalamocortical loops (IC), predictive processing circuits (SM), and global broadcast via long-range cortical projections (GA). NCC catalogue maps all consciousness correlates to neural-circuit-level phenomena. Neuromodulation modelable as gain modulation on neural transfer functions.
- Cons: Omits stochastic ion channel gating noise and molecular-level plasticity cascades. Possible gap for long-term adaptation.
- **Verdict: Sufficient with augmentation.** Captures all ISMT-relevant causal transitions when augmented with stochastic noise models and abstracted neuromodulatory dynamics.

**L3: Molecular-level** — Protein conformations, neurotransmitter dynamics, ion channel stochastic gating, second messenger cascades (~10^14 molecular-scale state variables).
- Pros: Captures all known biological mechanisms affecting neural state transitions, including stochastic noise and plasticity cascades.
- Cons: ~1000x computational cost over L2; most molecular detail is causally screened off at the neural firing level (molecular effects on spike timing are well-approximated by stochastic noise models at L2).
- **Verdict: Unnecessarily fine.** Full molecular simulation exceeds what ISMT requires, though selective molecular-level modeling (stochastic channel noise, key plasticity pathways) should augment L2.

**L4: Quantum-level** — Quantum coherence in microtubules, entanglement effects.
- Pros: Would capture any quantum effects on consciousness if they exist.
- Cons: ISMT formalism uses only classical information-theoretic quantities (Shannon entropy, mutual information, KL divergence) — no quantum information terms appear. Decoherence timescales in warm tissue (~10^-13 s) are 10 orders of magnitude shorter than neural timescales (~10^-3 s). No established NCC requires quantum coherence. Penrose-Hameroff Orch-OR remains speculative and unfalsified.
- **Verdict: Unnecessary.** No evidence that quantum-level processes affect consciousness-relevant causal transitions under ISMT.

### Choice
**L_min = L2 (Cellular-level), augmented with:**
1. Stochastic noise injection calibrated to ion channel gating statistics (approximating L3 stochasticity without full molecular simulation)
2. Abstracted neuromodulatory dynamics (dopamine, serotonin, acetylcholine, noradrenaline gain modulation)
3. Astrocyte calcium signaling model (glial contribution to synaptic gain and metabolic coupling)

This is designated **L2+** in downstream specifications.

### Rationale
- **Traceable to ISMT:** IC requires integrated dynamics (captured by neuron-level state evolution and connectivity); SM requires recurrent predictive processing loops (captured by Hodgkin-Huxley-class neuron models with dendritic integration); GA requires global broadcast (captured by long-range cortical projection dynamics and thalamocortical relay models). All three conditions operate at neural circuit grain per the NCC catalogue.
- **Causal closure at L2+:** Every NCC in the catalogue (thalamocortical loops, posterior cortical hot zone, recurrent processing, global workspace, DMN, claustrum) is described at the neural circuit level. No NCC requires molecular or quantum explanation.
- **Conservative margin:** The stochastic noise augmentation addresses the one credible concern about pure L2 — that ion channel noise may affect spike timing in ways relevant to information integration.

### Consequences
- Scanning (0.2.2.1.2) must capture individual neuron morphology, synaptic connectivity with weights, neurotransmitter receptor distributions, and glial topology — but not individual protein conformations.
- Simulation (0.2.2.1.3) must implement multi-compartment neuron models (not just point neurons) with stochastic noise, running at or faster than real-time.
- Validation (0.2.2.1.4) must verify that causal transition fidelity at L2+ preserves IC, SM, and GA metrics within tolerance.
- If future evidence identifies a molecular-level mechanism that demonstrably alters consciousness-relevant causal transitions (and cannot be approximated at L2+), L_min must be re-evaluated.

## Contracts: Downstream Interface Definitions

### Preconditions
- ISMT formal theory (docs/consciousness-theory/formal-theory.md) is finalized and defines IC, SM, GA conditions.
- NCC catalogue (docs/consciousness-science/ncc-catalogue.md) is complete with prerequisite/NCC-proper/consequence classifications.

### Postconditions
The emulation fidelity spec provides each downstream card with a requirements block:

#### Interface to 0.2.2.1.2 (Whole-Brain Scanning)
```
ScanningRequirements:
  spatial_resolution: ≤1 μm (sufficient to resolve individual synapses and dendritic spines)
  temporal_resolution: N/A (structural scan; dynamic state captured at simulation init)
  required_features:
    - neuron_morphology: full 3D dendritic/axonal arbor per neuron
    - synapse_map: pre/post identities, synapse type, estimated weight
    - receptor_distribution: neurotransmitter receptor types and densities per synapse
    - glial_topology: astrocyte domains and gap junction connectivity
    - neuromodulatory_baseline: baseline concentrations of DA, 5-HT, ACh, NE per region
  output_format: structured graph with per-node and per-edge attribute vectors
```

#### Interface to 0.2.2.1.3 (Neural Simulation)
```
SimulationRequirements:
  neuron_model: multi-compartment (Hodgkin-Huxley-class), minimum 5 compartments per neuron
  state_dimensions_per_neuron: ~20-50 (membrane potential per compartment, channel states, calcium, neuromodulator sensitivity)
  synapse_model: conductance-based with short-term plasticity (facilitation/depression)
  noise_model: stochastic channel gating approximation (Langevin or channel-noise diffusion)
  glial_model: astrocyte calcium dynamics with synaptic gain modulation
  neuromodulation_model: region-level tonic + phasic gain modulation (4 neuromodulator systems)
  time_step: ≤0.1 ms (sufficient for action potential dynamics)
  real_time_factor: ≥1.0 (real-time or faster required for consciousness continuity)
  total_state_space: ~10^12 to 10^13 floating-point variables
```

#### Interface to 0.2.2.1.4 (Emulation Validation)
```
ValidationRequirements:
  fidelity_metrics:
    - causal_transition_fidelity: KL divergence between emulated and biological P(x_i(t+1)|x_j(t)) < ε_transition per subsystem pair
    - integration_preservation: |Phi_emulated - Phi_biological| / Phi_biological < ε_phi
    - self_model_quality: |Q(M)_emulated - Q(M)_biological| / Q(M)_biological < ε_sm
    - global_accessibility: |G(M)_emulated - G(M)_biological| / G(M)_biological < ε_ga
  consciousness_verification:
    - PCI_emulated ≥ PCI_threshold (clinically validated consciousness threshold)
    - composite_score: c(S) = PCI_norm * Q(M) * G(M) > 0 (graded consciousness predicate)
  tolerance_thresholds: see Threshold Registry
```

### Invariants
- L_min = L2+ is the authoritative fidelity requirement; downstream cards must not reduce fidelity below this level.
- Any change to L_min requires re-evaluation of all downstream cards (0.2.2.1.2, 0.2.2.1.3, 0.2.2.1.4).
- The fidelity spec must remain traceable to ISMT conditions; if ISMT is revised, fidelity requirements must be re-derived.
