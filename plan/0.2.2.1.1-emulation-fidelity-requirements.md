# 0.2.2.1.1 Emulation Fidelity Requirements [DONE]

## Description

Determine the minimum resolution and fidelity at which a biological brain must be emulated to preserve subjective experience. This is the foundational question for all brain emulation work — the answer dictates the requirements for scanning (0.2.2.1.2), simulation (0.2.2.1.3), and validation (0.2.2.1.4).

Possible fidelity levels (from coarsest to finest):
- **Connectome-level**: neuron types, synaptic weights, network topology
- **Cellular-level**: individual neuron dynamics, dendritic computation, glial interactions
- **Molecular-level**: protein conformations, neurotransmitter dynamics, ion channel states
- **Quantum-level**: quantum coherence effects in microtubules or other structures

The required level depends entirely on what the F1 theory of consciousness (from 0.1.1) identifies as the physical substrate of subjective experience.

## Deliverable

A specification document (`docs/emulation-fidelity-spec.md`) that downstream sibling cards consume as their input requirements.

## Acceptance Criteria
- F1 theory of consciousness (0.1.1) reviewed; each fidelity level evaluated against what F1 identifies as necessary substrates for subjective experience
- Minimum necessary simulation resolution formally specified as one of the four fidelity levels, with justification traceable to F1
- Formal argument establishing why each lower-fidelity level is insufficient (or an argument that it is sufficient, if so)
- Upper and lower bounds on computational requirements (FLOPS, memory, bandwidth) estimated for each fidelity level for a human-scale brain (~86 billion neurons)
- Output document consumable by sibling cards 0.2.2.1.2, 0.2.2.1.3, and 0.2.2.1.4 as their requirements input
