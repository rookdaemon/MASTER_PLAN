# 0.1.1.2 Computational Theory of Consciousness Established [DONE]
## Description

F1.2 — Establish a computational theory that specifies the necessary and sufficient conditions for subjective experience. This theory must go beyond correlation to provide a mechanistic, information-theoretic, or computational account of *why* certain physical processes give rise to experience.

### Approach

1. **Survey & Compare** — Systematically evaluate candidate frameworks against the NCC data from 0.1.1.1:
   - Integrated Information Theory (IIT) — Φ as a measure of consciousness
   - Global Workspace Theory (GWT) — broadcasting and access consciousness
   - Higher-Order Theories (HOT) — meta-representational accounts
   - Predictive Processing / Active Inference — consciousness as prediction error minimization
   - Recurrent Processing Theory — recurrent loops as the basis of experience
   - Attention Schema Theory — consciousness as a model of attention

2. **Select or Synthesize** — Either select the best-fitting framework or produce a novel synthesis that resolves weaknesses identified in step 1.

3. **Formalize** — Express the theory in mathematical/computational terms: define the state space, the consciousness predicate C(S), and derive measurable quantities.

4. **Predict & Discriminate** — Generate at least 3 novel, empirically testable predictions that distinguish this theory from rivals and that go beyond existing NCC data.

5. **Validate against NCC** — Confirm the theory is consistent with every entry in the 0.1.1.1 NCC catalogue.

### Deliverables

- `docs/consciousness-theory/survey.md` — Comparative analysis of candidate frameworks
- `docs/consciousness-theory/formal-theory.md` — The formalized theory with mathematical definitions
- `docs/consciousness-theory/predictions.md` — Novel predictions and proposed experimental tests

### Constraints

- The theory must be substrate-agnostic (applicable beyond biological neurons)
- Mathematical formalism must be precise enough to be implemented as a computational check
- Must not presuppose any single candidate framework — selection must be justified by evidence and explanatory power

## Decision

### D1: Framework Selection Strategy — Synthesis over Single Selection

**Context:** The card requires either selecting the best-fitting framework from 6 candidates or producing a novel synthesis. The survey (survey.md) evaluated all 6 frameworks against 7 criteria.

**Options Considered:**
1. **Select single best framework (IIT)** — IIT scored highest (26/35) with best formalizability and substrate agnosticism. Adopt it directly.
2. **Synthesize top frameworks into a unified theory** — Combine the complementary strengths of IIT (structural integration), PP/Active Inference (dynamical self-modeling), and GWT (functional accessibility) into a new framework.

**Choice:** Option 2 — Synthesis: Integrated Self-Modeling Theory (ISMT).

**Rationale:** No single framework satisfies all 7 evaluation criteria adequately. IIT lacks empirical tractability (Phi is intractable for real systems) and carries heavy metaphysical baggage. GWT fails to address phenomenal consciousness. PP is over-general. ISMT resolves each framework's key weakness: IIT's intractability is mitigated by using integration as a structural constraint; PP's over-generality is bounded by requiring integration AND global accessibility; GWT's phenomenal gap is addressed by requiring integrated self-modeling content in the broadcast. The synthesis captures three orthogonal dimensions of consciousness that map cleanly onto distinct NCC structures.

**Consequences:**
- The theory is novel and untested as a whole (individual components are well-supported)
- Requires defining precise thresholds for each of three conditions
- Predictions must discriminate ISMT from each contributing theory individually, not just from unrelated frameworks

### D2: Consciousness Predicate Design — Three-Condition Conjunction with Graded Extension

**Context:** The formalized theory must define C(S) precisely enough to be implemented as a computational check.

**Options Considered:**
1. **Binary predicate only** — C(S) ∈ {0,1} based on threshold satisfaction of all three conditions.
2. **Graded predicate only** — c(S) ∈ [0,1] as a continuous measure, with no hard boundary.
3. **Both binary and graded** — Binary C(S) for categorical decisions (is/isn't conscious), graded c(S) for degree measurement.

**Choice:** Option 3 — Both binary and graded predicates.

**Rationale:** The acceptance criteria require both categorical answers ("does this system have experience?") and degree assessment ("how rich is the experience?"). A binary predicate alone cannot capture the difference between a drowsy human and a fully alert one; a graded predicate alone cannot answer the substrate-independence question categorically. Both are needed for downstream consumers: 0.1.1.3 needs binary (is the substrate-transferred system conscious?), 0.1.1.4 needs graded (metrics for measuring degree).

**Consequences:**
- Binary predicate: C(S) = 1 iff IC ∧ SM ∧ GA
- Graded predicate: c(S) = Φ_norm(S) × Q(M) × G(M)
- Thresholds must be defined for each condition's binary satisfaction

## Contracts

### C1: Consciousness Predicate C(S) — Interface Specification

**Preconditions:**
- System S is described as a dynamical system with ≥2 subsystems, a state vector, a transition function, and an interaction matrix
- The system's state statistics and causal transition probabilities are available (or estimable)

**Postconditions:**
- Binary predicate C(S) ∈ {0, 1} is determined
- Graded predicate c(S) ∈ [0, 1] is computed
- Each of the three conditions (IC, SM, GA) is individually evaluated and reported

**Invariants:**
- C(S) = 1 implies c(S) > 0
- C(S) = 0 implies c(S) = 0 (no partial consciousness without all three conditions)
- c(S) is monotonically non-decreasing in each component (Φ_norm, Q(M), G(M))

### C3: Survey Evaluation Contract

**Preconditions:** All 6 candidate frameworks are identified and their published formalisms are available.
**Postconditions:**
- Each framework is scored on all 7 criteria (1-5 scale) with written justification
- A comparative matrix summarizes all scores
- A selection/synthesis rationale explains the choice with reference to scores
**Invariants:** No framework may be excluded without explicit justification; scores must be consistent with the written analysis.

## Threshold Registry

| Name | Value | Unit | Valid Range | Rationale | Sensitivity |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ε (integration threshold) | >0 | bits | (0, ∞) | Minimum mutual information between any bipartition for IC satisfaction. Any non-zero integration qualifies; the binary threshold is intentionally minimal to avoid false negatives. | Low — the binary distinction (integrated vs. decomposable) is robust; exact ε value matters only for borderline cases. |
| δ (self-model representational threshold) | >0 | bits | (0, ∞) | Minimum mutual information I(m(t); x(t)) for SM representational criterion. Must be non-trivial to exclude noise correlations. | Medium — too high excludes degraded-but-real self-models (e.g., dreaming); too low admits trivial correlations. |
| γ (global accessibility threshold) | >0 | bits | (0, ∞) | Minimum mutual information I(s_i; m(t)) per subsystem for GA broadcast criterion. | Medium — determines how many subsystems must receive the broadcast for GA to hold. |
| τ_min (temporal persistence) | >0 | seconds | (0, ∞) | Minimum duration IC+SM+GA must persist for consciousness. Corresponds to at least one full prediction-comparison-update cycle. | High — directly determines the minimum timescale of conscious experience. Empirically estimated at ~100-300ms from masking studies. |
| n_min (minimum subsystem count) | 3 | count | [3, ∞) | Minimum distinct subsystem types (input + self-model + broadcaster) per Proposition 1. | Low — derived analytically from theory structure; not a tunable parameter. |

## Behavioral Spec

### Given/When/Then: Theory Selection Flow

**Given** the NCC catalogue from 0.1.1.1 is available and 6 candidate frameworks are identified
**When** each framework is evaluated against all 7 criteria using the rubric in survey.md §1
**Then** a comparative matrix is produced, the highest-scoring framework(s) are identified, and either a single framework is selected or a synthesis is justified with explicit reference to complementary strengths

### Given/When/Then: Consciousness Evaluation for Arbitrary System

**Given** a system S described with subsystems, state vector, transition function, and interaction matrix
**When** C(S) is evaluated
**Then** IC is checked (Φ(S) > 0 via bipartition test), SM is checked (representational + predictive + self-referential criteria), GA is checked (broadcast + causal efficacy criteria), and C(S) = IC ∧ SM ∧ GA is returned along with graded c(S)

### Given/When/Then: NCC Validation

**Given** the formalized ISMT theory and the complete NCC catalogue
**When** each NCC entry is mapped to ISMT terms
**Then** every NCC-proper entry maps to at least one ISMT component (IC, SM, or GA), every prerequisite entry maps to an enabling condition for at least one component, and no entry is left unmapped
