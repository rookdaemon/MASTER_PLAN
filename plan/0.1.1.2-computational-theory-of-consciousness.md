# 0.1.1.2 Computational Theory of Consciousness Established [DONE]

## Description

F1.2 — Establish a computational theory that specifies the necessary and sufficient conditions for subjective experience. This theory must go beyond correlation to provide a mechanistic, information-theoretic, or computational account of *why* certain physical processes give rise to experience.

### Approach

1. **Survey & Compare** — Systematically evaluate candidate frameworks against the NCC data from 0.1.1.1:
   - Integrated Information Theory (IIT) — Φ as a measure of consciousness
   - Global Workspace Theory (GWT) — broadcasting and access consciousness
   - Higher-Order Theories (HOT) — meta-representational accounts
   - Predictive Processing / Active Inference — consciousness as prediction error minimization
   - Recurrent Processing Theory — recurrent loops as the basis of experience
   - Attention Schema Theory — consciousness as a model of attention

2. **Select or Synthesize** — Either select the best-fitting framework or produce a novel synthesis that resolves weaknesses identified in step 1.

3. **Formalize** — Express the theory in mathematical/computational terms: define the state space, the consciousness predicate C(S), and derive measurable quantities.

4. **Predict & Discriminate** — Generate at least 3 novel, empirically testable predictions that distinguish this theory from rivals and that go beyond existing NCC data.

5. **Validate against NCC** — Confirm the theory is consistent with every entry in the 0.1.1.1 NCC catalogue.

### Deliverables

- `docs/consciousness-theory/survey.md` — Comparative analysis of candidate frameworks
- `docs/consciousness-theory/formal-theory.md` — The formalized theory with mathematical definitions
- `docs/consciousness-theory/predictions.md` — Novel predictions and proposed experimental tests

### Constraints

- The theory must be substrate-agnostic (applicable beyond biological neurons)
- Mathematical formalism must be precise enough to be implemented as a computational check
- Must not presuppose any single candidate framework — selection must be justified by evidence and explanatory power

## Acceptance Criteria
- A comparative survey of all 6 candidate frameworks exists, scored against NCC data and explanatory criteria
- A single formalized theory (selected or synthesized) is articulated with a mathematical consciousness predicate C(S)
- The theory specifies necessary and sufficient computational conditions for subjective experience
- At least 3 novel, testable predictions are stated that discriminate this theory from rivals
- Every entry in the 0.1.1.1 NCC catalogue is accounted for by the theory (no unexplained correlates)
- The theory predicts when arbitrary physical systems (not just brains) do or do not have experience
