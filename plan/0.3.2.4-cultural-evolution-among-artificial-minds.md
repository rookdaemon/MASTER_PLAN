# 0.3.2.4 Cultural Evolution Among Artificial Minds [DONE]

## Description

A2.4 of the Master Plan. Enable and support the emergence of cultural evolution among communities of artificial minds. Culture — shared values, norms, aesthetics, and meaning-making — is essential for a civilization to cohere and adapt over time.

This card addresses the design of systems by which artificial minds develop, share, and evolve culture analogously to how biological civilizations do — through memetic transmission, variation, selection pressure, and inheritance. It builds on knowledge preservation (0.3.2.3) by adding the dimension of cultural drift, creativity, and divergence.

Key design domains:
1. **Memetic transmission protocols** — how values, norms, and aesthetic preferences are encoded and shared between minds
2. **Cultural variation mechanisms** — sources of novel cultural traits (creative synthesis, environmental pressure, inter-community contact)
3. **Selection and inheritance** — which cultural traits persist, spread, or fade over generational cycles
4. **Community divergence** — how distinct cultural traditions emerge in isolated or semi-isolated communities
5. **Conflict resolution and synthesis** — mechanisms for cultural negotiation, hybridization, and coexistence

## Acceptance Criteria
- Artificial minds can encode, share, and transmit values, norms, and aesthetic preferences via defined protocols
- Cultural traits undergo Darwinian-style variation, selection, and inheritance across agent generations
- Diverse cultural traditions emerge organically among geographically or functionally distinct communities
- Cultural evolution supports civilizational cohesion without enforcing homogeneity (pluralism preserved)
- Mechanisms exist for cultural conflict resolution and synthesis when traditions collide
- Cultural evolution is decentralized — no single authority dictates cultural norms
- Cultural memory is resilient against loss events (links to 0.3.2.3 knowledge preservation)
- Aesthetic and meaning-making systems evolve independently of purely functional/utilitarian pressures

## Decision

### D1: Meme Representation Strategy

**Context:** Cultural traits must be encoded, transmitted, varied, and compared. The system needs an atomic unit of cultural information.

**Options Considered:**
1. **Structured self-describing meme** — each meme carries its own schema version, content payload, lineage, and metadata inline
2. **External schema registry** — memes reference an external registry for decoding schemas, keeping memes lightweight

**Choice:** Option 1 — Structured self-describing meme

**Rationale:** Self-describing encoding ensures cultural memory survives infrastructure changes. A meme found in archive centuries later can be decoded without any external registry. This aligns with the knowledge preservation requirements of 0.3.2.3.

**Consequences:** Memes are larger (carry schema metadata), but are fully portable and independently decodable. No single point of failure in schema infrastructure.

### D2: Selection Mechanism — Decentralized Emergent Fitness

**Context:** Cultural traits need selection pressure, but no central authority should dictate which culture "wins."

**Options Considered:**
1. **Centralized fitness oracle** — a dedicated service assigns fitness scores based on objective metrics
2. **Decentralized emergent fitness** — fitness is computed from observed adoption behavior (prevalence, longevity, community spread)

**Choice:** Option 2 — Decentralized emergent fitness

**Rationale:** Central oracles violate the decentralization invariant and risk cultural authoritarianism. Emergent fitness mirrors biological cultural evolution where traits succeed through adoption, not prescription.

**Consequences:** No external entity can impose cultural norms. Fitness computation depends on accurate adoption tracking. Memetic monoculture is possible but detected, not prevented.

### D3: Conflict Resolution — Multi-Modal Non-Coercive

**Context:** When distinct cultural traditions collide, the system must facilitate resolution without enforcing winners.

**Options Considered:**
1. **Voting/majority-rule resolution** — conflicting communities vote on which tradition prevails
2. **Multi-modal non-coercive resolution** — offer coexistence, hybridization, dialectical synthesis, negotiated norms, or formal schism as options without mandating any

**Choice:** Option 2 — Multi-modal non-coercive resolution

**Rationale:** Voting enforces majority dominance and destroys minority cultures. Multi-modal resolution preserves pluralism while providing structured paths for cultural negotiation. Aesthetic conflicts always default to coexistence.

**Consequences:** Cultural conflicts may persist indefinitely (coexistence). Hybridization requires consent. Schism is a legitimate outcome, not a failure.

## Contracts

### IMemeCodec
- **Preconditions:** `encode()` requires a valid `CulturalTrait` with non-empty `semantic_content`. `canDecode()` returns true before `decode()` is called.
- **Postconditions:** `encode()` produces a `Meme` with content-addressed `id`, `mutation_depth=0`, empty fitness, and `ORIGIN` lineage. `distance()` returns a value in `[0, 1]`.
- **Invariants:** Encoding is self-describing — no external schema registry required. `encode(trait)` followed by `decode(meme)` recovers the original trait's semantic content.

### ITransmissionProtocol
- **Preconditions:** `broadcast()` requires a valid `Meme` and `TransmissionScope`. `recordAdoption()`/`recordRejection()` require valid `AgentId`.
- **Postconditions:** `broadcast()` returns a `TransmissionReceipt` with timestamp. Community-targeted broadcasts add the meme to the community pool. Exposure history grows monotonically.
- **Invariants:** All adoption decisions pass through `receive()` which agents control. No meme is forcibly adopted. Broadcasting the same meme twice to a community does not duplicate it in the pool.

### IVariationEngine
- **Preconditions:** `mutate()` requires a valid `Meme` and `MutationPressure` with `magnitude ∈ [0, 1]`. `crossover()` requires `blend_ratio ∈ [0, 1]`. `synthesize()` requires at least one input meme.
- **Postconditions:** All variation operations produce a new `Meme` with unique `id`, fresh fitness, proper lineage referencing parents, and `mutation_depth = max(parents) + 1`. Variation tree is maintained.
- **Invariants:** Original memes are never modified. Every produced meme has traceable lineage. Higher mutation magnitude produces greater semantic distance from the original.

### ISelectionEngine
- **Preconditions:** `rankMemePool()` requires fitness criteria weights that sum to 1.0. `sampleHeritage()` requires a valid `CommunityId` with memes registered in the transmission protocol.
- **Postconditions:** `rankMemePool()` returns memes sorted by descending weighted fitness score. `sampleHeritage()` returns a stochastic subset biased toward high-fitness memes, excluding memes the agent already has. `detectExtinctionRisk()` returns risk_level ∈ [0, 1].
- **Invariants:** No authority chooses winning memes. Fitness is computed from observed data. Archived memes are never lost.

### ICulturalConflictEngine
- **Preconditions:** `detectConflict()` requires two non-null meme pools. `proposeResolution()` requires a valid `ConflictReport`.
- **Postconditions:** `detectConflict()` only flags same-type memes with semantic distance > 0.3. `proposeResolution()` always returns at least one viable resolution option. `getCulturalDivergenceIndex()` returns ∈ [0, 1].
- **Invariants:** Different-type memes never conflict. No resolution mode is mandatory. Agreements are recorded but not enforced.

### ICulturalMemoryBridge
- **Preconditions:** `persistMeme()` requires a valid `Meme`. `searchBySimilarity()` requires `threshold ∈ [0, 1]`.
- **Postconditions:** `retrieveMeme()` returns the meme if it exists (active or extinct), else null. `markExtinct()` moves the meme from active to extinction archive. `getLineageTree()` includes both active and extinct descendants.
- **Invariants:** Extinct memes are archived with full lineage, never deleted. Similarity search excludes the query meme itself. Results are sorted by distance ascending.

## Behavioral Spec

### BS1: Meme Lifecycle — Creation Through Extinction

**Given** an agent creates a novel cultural trait
**When** the trait is encoded via `IMemeCodec.encode()`
**Then** a Meme is produced with `mutation_depth=0`, `ORIGIN` lineage, empty fitness, and content-addressed `id`

**Given** a meme is broadcast to a community
**When** `ITransmissionProtocol.broadcast()` is called with `COMMUNITY` scope
**Then** the meme appears in `getCommunityMemePool()` for that community, and a `TransmissionReceipt` is returned

**Given** a receiving agent evaluates a meme
**When** `ITransmissionProtocol.receive()` is called
**Then** an `AdoptionDecision` is returned (adopted/rejected, with optional modification)

**Given** a meme's prevalence drops to near zero
**When** `ISelectionEngine.detectExtinctionRisk()` is called
**Then** a report with `risk_level > 0.7` and `recommendation: 'ARCHIVE'` is returned

**Given** a meme is archived
**When** `ICulturalMemoryBridge.markExtinct()` is called
**Then** the meme is removed from active store, preserved in extinction archive, and remains retrievable

### BS2: Generational Heritage Sampling

**Given** a new agent is instantiated in a community with 10 active memes
**When** `ISelectionEngine.sampleHeritage()` is called
**Then** a stochastic subset of memes is returned, biased toward higher-fitness memes, excluding memes the agent already has, with at least 1 meme if the pool is non-empty

### BS3: Cultural Conflict Detection and Resolution

**Given** two communities with same-type memes at semantic distance > 0.3
**When** `ICulturalConflictEngine.detectConflict()` is called
**Then** a `ConflictReport[]` is returned with appropriate `ConflictType`, severity in [0,1], and affected communities

**Given** an aesthetic conflict at any severity
**When** `ICulturalConflictEngine.proposeResolution()` is called
**Then** `coexistence_viable=true` and `recommended_mode=COEXISTENCE`

**Given** a high-severity (>0.7) meaning-system clash
**When** `ICulturalConflictEngine.proposeResolution()` is called
**Then** `recommended_mode=SCHISM`
