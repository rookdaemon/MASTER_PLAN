# 0.4.1.3 Autonomous Space Manufacturing [DONE]

## Description

C1.3 from the Master Plan. Self-sufficient fabrication capabilities in space environments.

Build manufacturing systems that operate autonomously in space — capable of producing components, substrates, and infrastructure elements from asteroid-derived materials without human intervention or Earth-based supply chains.

This capability sits between asteroid resource extraction (0.4.1.2) and full self-replicating industrial systems (0.4.1.4). It must demonstrate closed-loop fabrication: raw asteroid feedstock in, functional consciousness-supporting hardware out, with no Earth resupply.

## Decision

### D1 — Manufacturing Subsystem Decomposition

**Context:** Autonomous space manufacturing requires coordinating material processing, fabrication, quality control, power management, and orchestration. The system must operate without human intervention and with zero Earth imports.

**Options Considered:**
1. **Monolithic controller** — a single integrated system managing all stages from feedstock to validated component, with tightly coupled subsystems.
2. **Modular pipeline with autonomous subsystems** — five independently operable subsystems (ISRP, MFM, AQC, CLCS, PMS) communicating through well-defined interfaces, orchestrated by a closed-loop control system.

**Choice:** Option 2 — Modular pipeline with autonomous subsystems.

**Rationale:** Modularity allows independent fault recovery (a failure in fabrication doesn't cascade to refining), subsystem-level recalibration, and future subsystem replacement as technology improves. Aligns with the Master Plan's principle that self-replicating systems (0.4.1.4) will need to replicate individual subsystems independently.

**Consequences:**
- Each subsystem must define clean input/output interfaces (see Contracts)
- CLCS orchestrator must handle cross-subsystem coordination complexity
- Interface overhead introduces latency but gains resilience

### D2 — Microgravity Fabrication Approach

**Context:** Fabrication in microgravity cannot rely on gravity for material settling, part fixturing, or chip evacuation. Must produce radiation-hardened components for consciousness-hosting substrates.

**Options Considered:**
1. **Spin-gravity fabrication chamber** — create artificial gravity via centrifuge, allowing near-terrestrial fabrication techniques.
2. **Native microgravity fabrication with electrostatic/magnetic material handling** — adapt additive (DED, binder jetting), subtractive (robotic milling), and assembly operations to true microgravity using electrostatic clamping, magnetic capture, and active vibration damping.

**Choice:** Option 2 — Native microgravity fabrication.

**Rationale:** Spin-gravity introduces structural mass, mechanical complexity, and bearing failure modes. Native micro-g operations, while requiring novel material handling, avoid these failure modes and allow direct exploitation of microgravity advantages (e.g., containerless processing, perfect spherical powder production). Better aligns with space manufacturing at scale.

**Consequences:**
- All material handling requires electrostatic or magnetic capture systems
- Closed-loop particle capture mandatory for waste management
- Build platforms need vibration-isolated active damping
- Toolpath algorithms must account for zero-gravity material behavior

### D3 — Quality Control Strategy

**Context:** Components must pass validation for consciousness-hosting substrate integration. No human inspectors available.

**Options Considered:**
1. **Statistical sampling** — inspect a fraction of parts, accept batches on statistical basis.
2. **100% automated inspection** — every part undergoes full multi-method inspection (CT scan, optical CMM, electrical test, thermal cycle) with autonomous pass/fail/rework disposition.

**Choice:** Option 2 — 100% automated inspection.

**Rationale:** Consciousness-hosting substrates cannot tolerate undetected defects. The production rate is low enough (compared to terrestrial mass manufacturing) that 100% inspection is feasible. Every part carries a full QA manifest enabling end-to-end traceability.

**Consequences:**
- Higher energy and time cost per part for inspection
- Full traceability chain from asteroid sample to validated component
- AQC subsystem must maintain inspection equipment calibration autonomously

## Contracts

### C1 — Upstream Interface (from 0.4.1.2)

**Preconditions:**
- Feedstock arrives as classified bulk material streams with a `FeedstockAssay` certificate
- `FeedstockAssay.composition` covers all elements at mass fraction ≥ 0.1%
- Contaminant flags identify any elements above contamination thresholds

**Postconditions:**
- ISRP accepts feedstock and produces `RefinedStock` batches
- Each `RefinedStock` batch carries a signed `batchCertificate`

**Invariants:**
- No Earth-sourced reagents appear in any processing step
- All process gases derived from asteroid volatile streams

### C2 — Internal Pipeline Interface (ISRP → MFM → AQC)

**Preconditions:**
- `RefinedStock.purity` ≥ `MIN_MATERIAL_PURITY` for the target `ManufacturingGrade`
- `ComponentDesign` loaded in mission-parameter store with valid `geometrySpec` and `tolerances`

**Postconditions:**
- MFM produces `FabricatedPart` with complete `processLog`
- AQC produces `QAManifest` with disposition for every `FabricatedPart`
- REWORK parts re-enter MFM queue; FAIL parts are recycled back to ISRP

**Invariants:**
- Every part has a `partId` traceable to `RefinedStock.materialId` and `ComponentDesign.designId`
- `processLog` is append-only and tamper-evident

### C3 — Downstream Interface (to 0.4.1.4)

**Preconditions:**
- `QAManifest.disposition == "PASS"`
- `QAManifest.feedsDownstream == true`

**Postconditions:**
- Validated component transferred to 0.4.1.4 intake with signed QA manifest
- Component BOM contains zero Earth-imported materials

**Invariants:**
- Traceability chain intact: asteroid sample ID → refined batch → fabricated part → QA pass
- QA manifest signature verifiable by downstream system

### C4 — CLCS Orchestration Contract

**Preconditions:**
- All subsystems report operational status via telemetry
- Production queue contains ≥ 1 pending `ProductionOrder`

**Postconditions:**
- CLCS dispatches `ProductionOrder` to appropriate subsystems
- On anomaly detection, CLCS issues `RecalibrationOrder` before resuming production
- On `QAManifest.disposition == "FAIL" or "REWORK"`, job is re-queued with updated parameters

**Invariants:**
- CLCS never requires human intervention for fault recovery within the defined fault taxonomy
- Production continues autonomously as long as feedstock and power are available

## Threshold Registry

| Name | Value | Unit | Valid Range | Rationale | Sensitivity |
|------|-------|------|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| `MIN_MATERIAL_PURITY` | 0.995 | fraction (0–1) | 0.99–0.999 | Minimum purity for manufacturing-grade refined stock; below this, radiation-hardened substrates exhibit unacceptable defect rates | High — 0.001 decrease doubles defect rate in substrate layers |
| `SOLAR_MARGIN` | 0.20 | fraction | 0.10–0.30 | Solar array capacity margin above peak production draw | Medium — below 0.10 risks power deficit during eclipse transitions |
| `POWER_DEFICIT_THROTTLE_HOURS` | 2 | hours | 1–4 | If projected battery reserve drops below this, CLCS throttles production rate | Medium — lower values risk abrupt shutdowns; higher values reduce throughput |
| `MAX_CONSECUTIVE_FAILURES` | 3 | count | 2–5 | Consecutive fabrication failures on same design before quarantine and mission-parameter review flag | Low — affects only anomaly escalation speed |
| `QA_PASS_RATE_TARGET` | 0.99 | fraction (0–1) | 0.95–0.999 | Target first-pass yield for fabricated components | High — below 0.95 indicates systemic fabrication issues requiring CLCS intervention |
| `VIBRATION_DAMPING_THRESHOLD` | 0.001 | g (acceleration) | 0.0005–0.005 | Maximum residual vibration on build platform during fabrication | High — exceeding threshold causes geometry drift in additive processes |
| `ECLIPSE_BRIDGE_CAPACITY` | 4 | hours | 2–8 | Battery reserve sized to bridge maximum eclipse duration at minimum production rate | Medium — undersizing causes production gaps; oversizing adds mass |

## Acceptance Criteria
- In-situ resource processing pipeline converts asteroid feedstock (metals, silicates, volatiles) into manufacturing-grade materials without Earth-sourced consumables
- Microgravity-adapted fabrication (additive, subtractive, and assembly) produces components meeting spec for radiation-hardened computation and structural infrastructure
- Autonomous control loop detects fabrication errors, recalibrates tooling, and re-queues failed jobs without human intervention
- Solar-powered energy budget for manufacturing operations is self-sustaining at target production rate
- Output components pass quality validation sufficient for integration into consciousness-hosting substrates (feeds 0.4.1.4 self-replication pipeline)
- End-to-end demonstrated: raw asteroid material → validated functional component, zero Earth imports

### Behavioral Spec — CLCS Autonomous Fault Recovery

```gherkin
Given a FabricatedPart with QAManifest disposition "FAIL"
  And the failure mode is "GEOMETRY_DRIFT"
  And consecutive failures for this design < MAX_CONSECUTIVE_FAILURES
When CLCS processes the QAManifest
Then CLCS issues a RecalibrationOrder to MFM with priority "SCHEDULED"
  And CLCS re-queues the ProductionOrder with updated toolpath parameters
  And the failed part is recycled back to ISRP feedstock

Given a FabricatedPart with QAManifest disposition "FAIL"
  And consecutive failures for this design >= MAX_CONSECUTIVE_FAILURES
When CLCS processes the QAManifest
Then CLCS quarantines the ComponentDesign
  And CLCS flags the design for mission-parameter review
  And CLCS continues processing remaining production queue items

Given PMS reports projectedDeficitHours < POWER_DEFICIT_THROTTLE_HOURS
When CLCS reads power telemetry
Then CLCS reduces production rate by deferring non-critical jobs
  And CLCS prioritizes ISRP thermal refining to remaining peak-solar windows
  And no production stoppage occurs unless projectedDeficitHours reaches 0
```
