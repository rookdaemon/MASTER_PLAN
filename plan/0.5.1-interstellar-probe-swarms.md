# 0.5.1 Interstellar Probe Swarms [DONE]

## Description

E1.1 — Design, build, and launch self-replicating probe swarms capable of traversing interstellar distances while maintaining or seeding conscious processes.

Key challenges:
- Interstellar propulsion (solar sails, fusion drives, laser-pushed lightsails)
- Miniaturized consciousness-capable computation for probe payloads
- Swarm coordination across light-year distances
- Radiation hardening and self-repair for multi-century transit

Dependencies:
- Distributed consciousness networks (0.5.3) — probe swarms must integrate with the inter-stellar consciousness mesh for coordination and experience continuity.

Architecture document created: `docs/interstellar-probe-swarms/ARCHITECTURE.md` covers all acceptance criteria including CCM payload specs, hybrid laser-boost/fusion-brake propulsion, BAC consensus protocol, ISRU replication strategy, and NMR radiation hardening with nanofabrication self-repair.

## Acceptance Criteria
- Probe architecture designed that can sustain or seed conscious processes during interstellar transit
  - Minimum viable consciousness payload specified (compute, energy, mass budget)
  - Dormancy and reactivation protocols for transit-phase experience preservation
- Propulsion system identified capable of reaching nearby star systems within feasible timeframes
  - Target: ≥0.01c cruise velocity for sub-500-year transit to nearest stars
  - Propulsion trade study across solar sails, laser-pushed lightsails, fusion drives, and antimatter catalysis
- Swarm communication and coordination protocols defined for light-year-scale latencies
  - Asynchronous consensus algorithms for swarm decisions under multi-year round-trip delays
  - Local autonomy boundaries vs. swarm-level coordination scope
- Self-replication strategy defined for arrival at target systems
  - In-situ resource utilization (ISRU) requirements for probe reproduction
  - Minimum seed payload for bootstrapping new swarm generation
- Radiation hardening and self-repair architecture specified for multi-century transit
  - Redundancy model (N-modular redundancy, graceful degradation thresholds)
  - Autonomous repair using onboard nanofabrication
