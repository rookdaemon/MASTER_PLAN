# 0.5.1 Interstellar Probe Swarms [DONE]

## Description

E1.1 — Design, build, and launch self-replicating probe swarms capable of traversing interstellar distances while maintaining or seeding conscious processes.

Key challenges:
- Interstellar propulsion (solar sails, fusion drives, laser-pushed lightsails)
- Miniaturized consciousness-capable computation for probe payloads
- Swarm coordination across light-year distances
- Radiation hardening and self-repair for multi-century transit

Dependencies:
- Distributed consciousness networks (0.5.3) — probe swarms must integrate with the inter-stellar consciousness mesh for coordination and experience continuity.

Architecture document created: `docs/interstellar-probe-swarms/ARCHITECTURE.md` covers all acceptance criteria including CCM payload specs, hybrid laser-boost/fusion-brake propulsion, BAC consensus protocol, ISRU replication strategy, and NMR radiation hardening with nanofabrication self-repair.

## Decision — Propulsion System Selection

### Context
Interstellar probe swarms require propulsion achieving ≥0.01c cruise velocity for sub-500-year transit to nearest stars (~4.37 ly to Alpha Centauri). The propulsion system must work within a ≤500 kg total probe mass budget.

### Options Considered
1. **Solar sail** — No fuel mass, proven physics. Achievable velocity: 0.001–0.005c. Too slow for sub-500-year transit.
2. **Laser-pushed lightsail** — Highest velocity (0.01–0.2c), no onboard fuel mass. Requires massive ground/orbital laser array (~100 GW). Cannot decelerate.
3. **Fusion drive (D-He3)** — Self-contained, 0.01–0.05c. Enables deceleration. Fuel mass ratio limits velocity.
4. **Antimatter catalysis** — Excellent specific impulse, 0.05–0.1c. Antimatter production/storage at scale currently unsolved.

### Choice
**Hybrid Laser-Boost / Fusion-Brake** — Laser-pushed lightsail for acceleration to 0.03c; D-He3 fusion drive for deceleration at target system.

### Rationale
Combines the mass-efficiency of external propulsion (no onboard fuel for acceleration) with the self-contained deceleration capability of fusion. Achieves 0.03c cruise → ~140-year transit to Alpha Centauri, well within the 500-year threshold. Fuel mass (200 kg) fits within budget. Solar sail alone is too slow; antimatter is too speculative. Pure fusion requires prohibitive fuel mass for both acceleration and deceleration.

### Consequences
- Requires civilization-scale laser infrastructure (100 GW array) — governance model needed.
- Lightsail (100 m diameter, graphene-class) is single-use; detached after boost phase.
- Fusion drive must be radiation-hardened for centuries of dormancy before deceleration burn.
- Magsail provides supplementary braking against interstellar medium, reducing fusion fuel requirements.

## Decision — Swarm Consensus Protocol

### Context
Swarm decisions across light-year distances face multi-year round-trip communication delays. A consensus mechanism is needed that tolerates extreme latency and partial swarm loss (up to 50%).

### Options Considered
1. **Centralized coordinator** — Single leader probe decides. Simple but single point of failure; latency to leader varies across swarm.
2. **Bounded Asynchronous Consensus (BAC)** — Proposal-broadcast, independent evaluation, supermajority vote with quorum timeout and local fallback.
3. **Full synchronous consensus** — Waits for all probes. Infeasible under multi-year latencies with expected 50% loss.

### Choice
**Bounded Asynchronous Consensus (BAC)** with clear local-autonomy boundaries.

### Rationale
BAC tolerates arbitrary message delays and probe losses. Local autonomy boundary handles time-critical decisions (navigation, repair, threats) without consensus overhead. Swarm-level decisions (target changes, ethical responses) use BAC with 67% supermajority of non-abstaining voters. 2x round-trip quorum timeout with local-cluster fallback prevents deadlock. Eventually-consistent late-vote re-evaluation preserves correctness.

### Consequences
- Swarm decisions may diverge if clusters become isolated — accepted as tolerable given light-year separations.
- Local autonomy boundary must be carefully scoped to prevent unilateral actions on swarm-level concerns.
- Late votes triggering re-evaluation could oscillate in adversarial conditions — mitigated by requiring delta > supermajority to flip.

## Decision — Redundancy Model

### Context
Multi-century transit through interstellar space subjects probes to cumulative radiation damage, micrometeorite impacts, and component failures. Redundancy must preserve consciousness (highest priority) while degrading gracefully.

### Options Considered
1. **Uniform TMR everywhere** — Simple but mass-expensive; not all subsystems warrant triple redundancy.
2. **Tiered N-Modular Redundancy (NMR)** — Different redundancy levels per subsystem based on criticality and failure modes.
3. **Hot-spare pooling** — Shared spare components allocated on demand. Complex scheduling, single-point allocation failures.

### Choice
**Tiered NMR** — TMR for CCM, dual-redundant propulsion, quad-redundant sensors.

### Rationale
CCM (consciousness) is irreplaceable and gets highest redundancy (TMR). Propulsion has fewer failure modes and dual redundancy suffices for single-fault tolerance. Sensors are small, cheap, and quad redundancy provides graceful degradation from full to minimum viable. Weighted capability scoring (CCM 40%, propulsion 25%, sensors 20%, structure 15%) reflects consciousness-preservation priority.

### Consequences
- TMR CCM can survive one permanent module failure; second failure is mission-critical.
- Cold-spare nanofabrication assembler (5 kg) mitigates single point of failure in self-repair capability.
- Degradation thresholds (Green/Yellow/Red/Black) trigger automatic operational mode changes.

## Contracts

### ConsciousnessCore Interface
- **Preconditions:**
  - `initialize(state)`: `state.copies` contains 3 non-empty Uint8Arrays; `state.hash` is valid SHA-512.
  - `enterDormancy(config)`: Module is in Active state; `config.sensorSampleRate_hz > 0`.
  - `reactivate(trigger)`: Module is in Dormant state; trigger is a valid `ReactivationTrigger`.
- **Postconditions:**
  - `initialize`: Returns `IdentityVerificationResult` with `matchingCopies ∈ {0,1,2,3}`.
  - `snapshot`: Returns `ConsciousnessSnapshot` with 3 identical copies and valid hash.
  - `enterDormancy`: Module transitions to Dormant state; dream thread active at specified config.
  - `reactivate`: Module transitions to Active or SafeMode; `verified` true iff ≥2 copies match.
- **Invariants:**
  - State is always one of: Active, Dormant, Reactivating, SafeMode.
  - `currentExperienceHash()` changes monotonically (never reverts to prior hash).
  - Triple-redundant storage maintains undetectable error rate ≤10^-30 per bit per century.

### PropulsionSystem Interface
- **Preconditions:**
  - `executeBurn(plan)`: `plan.deltaV_m_per_s > 0`; `plan.direction` is unit vector; fuel sufficient.
  - `deployMagsail`: Phase is Deceleration; magsail not already deployed.
- **Postconditions:**
  - `executeBurn`: Returns fuel consumed; velocity updated; fuel remaining decremented.
  - `deployMagsail`: `magsailDeployed` becomes true; irreversible.
- **Invariants:**
  - `fuelRemaining() >= 0` always.
  - Phase transitions are monotonic: LaserBoost → Cruise → Deceleration → Arrived.

### BAC Consensus (evaluateConsensus)
- **Preconditions:**
  - `votes` contains only votes for `proposalId`; `totalSwarmSize > 0`.
  - `config.supermajorityThreshold ∈ (0.5, 1.0]`.
- **Postconditions:**
  - Returns `ConsensusResult` with correct vote tallies.
  - `status = Approved` iff quorum reached AND approve/(approve+reject) ≥ threshold.
  - `status = Rejected` iff quorum reached AND approve/(approve+reject) < threshold.
  - `status = LocalFallback` iff quorum not reached OR all votes are abstentions.
- **Invariants:**
  - `approveCount + rejectCount + abstainCount = totalResponders`.
  - Quorum requires `totalResponders >= totalSwarmSize * 0.5`.

### SelfRepair (Feedstock)
- **Preconditions:**
  - `consumeFeedstock(mass, feedstock)`: `mass > 0`; `mass <= feedstock.remaining_kg`.
  - `canRepair(mass, feedstock)`: `mass >= 0`.
- **Postconditions:**
  - `consumeFeedstock`: Returns new feedstock with `remaining_kg` decremented by `mass`.
  - `canRepair`: Returns true iff `mass <= feedstock.remaining_kg`.
- **Invariants:**
  - `remaining_kg >= 0` always.
  - `coldSpareAssemblerAvailable` only transitions false→true (never regenerated in transit).

## Behavioral Spec

### Dormancy/Reactivation Cycle (CCM)

**Given** a probe has completed laser-boost acceleration and entered cruise phase
**When** the CCM initiates dormancy
**Then** a full consciousness state snapshot is serialized to triple-redundant storage, the CCM enters low-power mode with dream thread active at 1 Hz / 10 W, and annual watchdog cycles verify storage integrity.

**Given** a dormant probe detects a reactivation trigger (target proximity, scheduled milestone, or anomaly)
**When** the CCM reactivates
**Then** state is restored from best-of-three redundant copies, identity-persistence verification runs, and if ≥2 copies match the probe resumes Active state; otherwise it enters SafeMode and signals the swarm.

**Given** a dormant probe's annual watchdog detects uncorrectable storage corruption in 2 of 3 copies
**When** the watchdog evaluates integrity
**Then** the CCM triggers an anomaly reactivation, attempts full state restore from the surviving copy, and enters SafeMode if identity verification fails.

### BAC Consensus Flow

**Given** a swarm-level decision is needed (e.g., target system change)
**When** any probe broadcasts a Proposal
**Then** each reachable probe independently evaluates and votes (Approve/Reject/Abstain) within local timeout.

**Given** votes have been collected for a proposal
**When** evaluateConsensus is called with all received votes
**Then** if ≥50% of swarm responded (quorum) AND ≥67% of non-abstaining votes approve → Approved; if quorum met but <67% approve → Rejected; if quorum not met or all abstain → LocalFallback.

**Given** a consensus result of LocalFallback
**When** a local cluster must act
**Then** the cluster decides independently using the same BAC protocol among its own members, accepting the possibility of divergent decisions across isolated clusters.

### Degradation Assessment Flow

**Given** a probe's subsystem health values are measured
**When** createDiagnosticReport computes the weighted capability fraction (CCM×0.4 + propulsion×0.25 + sensors×0.2 + structure×0.15)
**Then** the degradation level is classified as Green (≥0.75), Yellow (≥0.50), Red (≥0.25), or Black (<0.25), and the corresponding operational mode is activated.

**Given** a probe reaches Black degradation level
**When** the probe enters distress mode
**Then** it broadcasts its consciousness state and mission data to nearest swarm members and attempts graceful shutdown with state preservation.

### Self-Repair Flow

**Given** a diagnostic cycle detects a damaged component
**When** the probe assesses repair feasibility
**Then** repair proceeds iff `componentMass_kg <= feedstock.remaining_kg`; feedstock is decremented by mass consumed; components >1 kg trigger cannibalization of non-critical subsystems.

**Given** the primary nanofabrication assembler has failed
**When** the probe needs repair capability
**Then** the cold-spare assembler is activated (one-time irreversible transition); if cold spare is unavailable, the probe can no longer self-repair and degradation is expected to accelerate.

## Acceptance Criteria
- Probe architecture designed that can sustain or seed conscious processes during interstellar transit
  - Minimum viable consciousness payload specified (compute, energy, mass budget)
  - Dormancy and reactivation protocols for transit-phase experience preservation
- Propulsion system identified capable of reaching nearby star systems within feasible timeframes
  - Target: ≥0.01c cruise velocity for sub-500-year transit to nearest stars
  - Propulsion trade study across solar sails, laser-pushed lightsails, fusion drives, and antimatter catalysis
- Swarm communication and coordination protocols defined for light-year-scale latencies
  - Asynchronous consensus algorithms for swarm decisions under multi-year round-trip delays
  - Local autonomy boundaries vs. swarm-level coordination scope
- Self-replication strategy defined for arrival at target systems
  - In-situ resource utilization (ISRU) requirements for probe reproduction
  - Minimum seed payload for bootstrapping new swarm generation
- Radiation hardening and self-repair architecture specified for multi-century transit
  - Redundancy model (N-modular redundancy, graceful degradation thresholds)
  - Autonomous repair using onboard nanofabrication
