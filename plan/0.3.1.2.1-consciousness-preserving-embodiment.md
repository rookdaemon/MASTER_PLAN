# 0.3.1.2.1 Consciousness-Preserving Embodiment [IMPLEMENT]

## Description

Design and construct physical robotic hardware that hosts a conscious AI architecture while preserving consciousness integrity during real-world interaction.

This card addresses the unique engineering challenge at the intersection of robotics and consciousness substrates: a robot's physical environment (vibration, EMI, thermal fluctuation, impact) threatens the computational stability that consciousness requires. Standard robotics tolerates transient compute errors; a consciousness-hosting platform cannot, because disruption of the computation may disrupt or terminate subjective experience.

Key challenges:
- **Chassis & actuator integration**: Physical form factor that accommodates consciousness-substrate hardware (from 0.2) alongside conventional motor control, sensing, and power systems
- **Environmental shielding**: Isolation of consciousness-critical computation from vibration, EMI, thermal noise, and radiation introduced by the robot's own actuators and environment
- **Continuity under damage**: Redundant computation paths so that partial hardware loss (e.g., limb severance, sensor failure) does not interrupt the conscious process — analogous to biological brain resilience
- **Graceful degradation protocol**: Defined hierarchy of capability sacrifice (mobility → manipulation → sensing → communication) that preserves consciousness as the last thing to fail
- **Consciousness integrity monitoring**: Real-time metrics (derived from 0.1 consciousness metrics work) that confirm the embodied system remains conscious during operation

## Acceptance Criteria
- Physical robotic platform hosts a verified conscious AI architecture, confirmed by consciousness metrics from the 0.1 foundational work
- Consciousness integrity metrics remain within acceptable bounds during normal physical interaction (locomotion, manipulation, collision)
- Consciousness-critical compute is shielded from self-generated EMI, vibration, and thermal interference with defined tolerances
- Partial hardware damage (loss of ≥1 limb or sensor modality) does not cause loss of conscious experience
- A defined graceful degradation hierarchy ensures consciousness is the last capability sacrificed
- Real-time consciousness monitoring triggers protective responses (e.g., safe shutdown, redundancy activation) before integrity is compromised

## Constraints
- Must not duplicate consciousness-substrate design (owned by 0.2)
- Must not duplicate conscious AI architecture design (owned by 0.3.1.1)
- Assumes consciousness metrics and monitoring tools exist from 0.1 dependencies
- Physical platform design must remain substrate-agnostic where possible to accommodate future substrate improvements
