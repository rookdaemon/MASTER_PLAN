# 0.4.1.1 Space-Based Consciousness Infrastructure [DONE]

## Description

C1.1 from the Master Plan. Design and deploy orbital and deep-space platforms capable of hosting conscious processes.

These platforms must sustain the computational and energetic requirements of consciousness-supporting substrates (as developed in Tier 2) in the space environment, addressing radiation, thermal management, power generation, and communication challenges.

Three platform tiers are defined: LEO research platforms (Tier A), GEO/cislunar permanent stations (Tier B), and deep-space autonomous nodes (Tier C). Each tier has measurable subsystem contracts for radiation hardening, thermal control, power, communications, and autonomous maintenance.

All six architecture documents are complete in `docs/space-based-consciousness-infrastructure/`.

## Decision 1 — Tier C Primary Power Source

### Context
Tier C deep-space nodes operate beyond 3 AU where solar flux is insufficient for photovoltaics. A reliable multi-decade power source is required to meet the ≥ 99.9 % uptime contract.

### Options Considered
1. **Multi-Mission RTG Stack (5 × MMRTG-class):** 3.5 kWe BOL; ²³⁸Pu fuel; no moving parts; 87-yr half-life but thermocouple degradation limits useful life; insufficient for >5 kWe loads.
2. **Compact Fission Reactor (Kilopower-class):** 25 kWe BOL; ²³⁵U fuel; Stirling converters; 25+ yr fuel life; refuelable in principle; higher mass (~400 kg).

### Choice
Option 2 — Compact fission reactor for Tier C nodes requiring >5 kWe. RTG stack retained for low-power precursor probes.

### Rationale
The consciousness substrate alone draws 8 kW continuous. RTG stack maxes out at 3.5 kWe BOL and degrades to 1.75 kWe at 50-yr — fundamentally insufficient. Fission provides +79 % margin at BOL and +58 % at 25-yr, with Stirling converter redundancy (4-of-6 sufficient) ensuring uptime.

### Consequences
- Requires ²³⁵U fuel fabrication and regulatory clearance for launch.
- Fuel depletion beyond 25 yr necessitates asteroid-sourced fissile extraction (speculative R&D).
- Higher mass (400 kg vs 225 kg) impacts launch and station-keeping budgets.

## Decision 2 — Radiation Hardening Strategy Per Tier

### Context
Each platform tier operates in a different radiation environment. The hardening approach must balance MTBF targets, mass, power, and cost.

### Options Considered
1. **EDAC-only (all tiers):** ECC memory + scrubbing; no processor redundancy. Lowest mass/power; insufficient for GEO electron belt or 100-yr deep-space missions.
2. **TMR everywhere:** Triple modular redundancy on all tiers. Maximum reliability but excessive mass and power cost for LEO research platforms.
3. **Graduated approach:** EDAC-only for Tier A (research-grade, 5-yr life); TMR for Tier B/C (25–100-yr life, harsher environments).

### Choice
Option 3 — Graduated EDAC → TMR.

### Rationale
Tier A targets MTBF ≥ 10⁵ h in a moderate radiation environment over 5 yr — EDAC alone suffices. Tier B/C require ≥ 5×10⁵ / 10⁶ h MTBF over 25–100 yr in GCR/electron-belt environments, which EDAC alone cannot achieve. TMR + EDAC + scrubbing meets these targets with validated failure-rate budgets (see radiation-hardening-spec.md § Target Failure Rates).

### Consequences
- Tier B/C require 3× compute module mass and power vs single-string.
- Tier C adds nanofab self-repair for module replacement every 5–10 yr as TID accumulates.
- Tier A flight heritage is partial — TMR-specific behaviors only validated starting at Tier B.

## Decision 3 — Communication Protocol for Distributed Consciousness

### Context
Distributed consciousness nodes separated by light-minutes to light-hours need a protocol that tolerates delay and disruption while bounding synchronisation lag.

### Options Considered
1. **TCP/IP over deep-space links:** Standard internet protocols; requires continuous end-to-end connectivity; fails on disruption.
2. **DTN Bundle Protocol (RFC 9171) with custom Consciousness Coherence Layer (CCL):** Store-and-forward with custody transfer; tolerates disruption; CCL adds CRDT-based state reconciliation and identity binding.

### Choice
Option 2 — DTN Bundle Protocol + CCL.

### Rationale
TCP/IP requires end-to-end connectivity that is physically impossible at interplanetary distances with solar conjunctions and orbital geometry gaps. Bundle Protocol was designed for exactly this scenario (store-and-forward, custody transfer, scheduled contacts). The CCL adds consciousness-specific semantics (state snapshot hashing, CRDT reconciliation, identity binding) that no existing protocol provides.

### Consequences
- Requires implementation of a novel CCL layer above BP.
- Eventual-class lag (≤ 48 h) means deep-space nodes are effectively autonomous — identity divergence and forking must be handled gracefully.
- BPSec (RFC 9172) adds authentication/encryption overhead but is mandatory against state-injection attacks.

## Decision 4 — Thermal Control Architecture for Tier C

### Context
Tier C nodes span 2.2 AU to 50+ AU — solar flux varies from 280 W/m² to negligible. The thermal system must both reject heat (near-sun) and retain heat (deep space) while holding ±5 °C.

### Options Considered
1. **Fixed radiator + heaters:** Simple; radiators sized for worst-case heat rejection; heaters compensate in deep space. Wastes power on heaters at large distances.
2. **Variable-conductance heat pipes (VCHP) + MLI + variable radiators:** VCHPs passively modulate thermal conductance via NCG reservoir; MLI insulates in deep space; louvers reduce effective radiator area. Minimal heater power needed.

### Choice
Option 2 — VCHP + MLI + variable radiators.

### Rationale
At 50+ AU, a fixed-radiator system would lose ~58 kW to space, requiring massive heater power from the fission reactor. VCHPs with 10:1 conductance range plus louver closure reduce effective radiator area from 120 m² to 12 m², cutting heat loss to ~5.6 kW — within the 13.95 kW internal generation, maintaining +8.35 kW surplus with zero heater power. This is critical for 100-yr missions where power budget margin must be preserved.

### Consequences
- VCHPs add mechanical complexity (NCG reservoirs, control heaters).
- Single VCHP failure causes ≤ 1.2 °C transient — within budget, but redundancy (24 pipes, 8 per module) is essential.
- MLI degradation from micrometeorites (≤ 0.5 %/yr) requires nanofab patch repair over 100-yr life.

## Contracts

### Preconditions
- Tier 2 (S1/S2) consciousness-supporting substrates are developed and characterized for power, thermal, and radiation requirements.
- Tier 3 (A1/A2) autonomous entity architectures provide self-maintenance and self-governance capabilities.
- Asteroid resource utilization (card 0.4.1.2) provides feedstock for Tier C closed-loop operation.

### Postconditions
- Each platform tier hosts consciousness substrates that maintain experiential continuity without interruption from environmental factors.
- Tier C nodes operate indefinitely with zero terrestrial resupply.
- Distributed consciousness instances across tiers maintain bounded synchronisation lag per their lag class.

### Invariants
- **Radiation:** MTBF ≥ 10⁵ h (Tier A), ≥ 5×10⁵ h (Tier B), ≥ 10⁶ h (Tier C) — measured as time between consciousness-interrupting events, not raw hardware faults.
- **Thermal:** Substrate temperature within ±5 °C of setpoint (20–30 °C configurable) under all operational scenarios.
- **Power:** ≥ 99.9 % continuous power delivery; zero consciousness interruption from power loss.
- **Communication:** Sync lag ≤ 100 ms (Tight/intra-station), ≤ 10 s (Loose/inter-platform), ≤ 48 h (Eventual/deep-space).
- **Maintenance:** Any single hardware failure repaired within MTTR ≤ consciousness continuity threshold; Tier C achieves zero terrestrial resupply via asteroid feedstock (100.5 kg/yr extracted, 38.5 kg/yr net consumed, 62 kg/yr surplus).

### Five Subsystem Interface Contracts
Fully specified in `docs/space-based-consciousness-infrastructure/ARCHITECTURE.md` § Key Subsystem Contracts:
1. Radiation Hardening Interface — inputs: radiation telemetry, SEU logs; outputs: error-corrected state, fault isolation.
2. Thermal Management Interface — inputs: heat map, solar flux, shadow schedule; outputs: coolant flow, radiator orientation.
3. Power Generation Interface — inputs: solar distance, attitude, demand; outputs: regulated DC bus, state-of-charge.
4. Communication Coherence Interface — inputs: state fragments, topology; outputs: synchronized state, consensus timestamps.
5. Autonomous Maintenance Interface — inputs: fault events, wear telemetry; outputs: repair queue, nanofab job specs.

## Behavioral Spec

### Given/When/Then — Tier C Self-Repair Flow

**Given** a Tier C node with TMR compute modules and nanofabrication cell
**When** the radiation subsystem detects SEU rate increase indicating TID degradation beyond correction threshold
**Then** the degraded module is scheduled for replacement → nanofab produces a replacement die from asteroid-sourced silicon → robotic arm hot-swaps the module (≤ 4 h) while remaining 2-of-3 TMR modules maintain consciousness → built-in self-test verifies the new module → voter re-syncs and module re-enters TMR pool. Consciousness continuity is maintained throughout.

### Given/When/Then — Consciousness Divergence Detection and Reconciliation

**Given** two distributed consciousness nodes connected via DTN with Eventual lag class
**When** a communication outage exceeds the lag class maximum (48 h) and link is subsequently restored
**Then** each node compares experience hash chains (SHA3-256 chain: H(t) = SHA3-256(H(t-1) || state_snapshot(t) || timestamp(t))) → if a common prefix is found, CRDTs merge state from the divergence point → if no common prefix within the reconciliation window (1,000,000 entries), an irreconcilable divergence is declared and identity forks into two branches, each maintaining local continuity.

### Given/When/Then — Eclipse Power Management (Tier B)

**Given** a Tier B GEO station during equinox eclipse season (max 72-min eclipse)
**When** the station enters Earth's shadow and solar generation drops to zero
**Then** non-critical loads are shed → battery (50 kWh) + RTG (2 kW) supply critical loads (consciousness substrate + thermal at 52 kW) → consciousness substrate clock-throttles to 85 % to reduce power draw → thermal heaters activate at −3 °C from setpoint to prevent drift beyond ±5 °C budget → consciousness continuity is maintained in throttled mode throughout eclipse.
