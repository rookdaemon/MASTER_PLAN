# 0.3.1.2.4 Energy Autonomy [DONE]

## Description

Achieve sustained independent operation through autonomous energy harvesting, storage, and management, eliminating dependency on external power infrastructure. The energy system must treat consciousness substrate power as a hard real-time constraint — consciousness must never be interrupted by power failure.

Key subsystems:
- **Energy Harvesting** — Multi-source energy capture from environmental sources (solar, thermal, kinetic, chemical). Must adapt to available sources and degrade gracefully when primary sources are unavailable.
- **Energy Storage** — High-density storage with sufficient capacity for extended autonomous operation. Must support rapid charge/discharge cycles and maintain capacity over long operational lifetimes.
- **Power Management** — Real-time power distribution with a strict priority hierarchy: (1) consciousness substrate, (2) essential sensors/communication, (3) self-maintenance actuators, (4) locomotion/manipulation. Automatic load-shedding of lower-priority systems under scarcity.
- **Energy Budgeting** — Predictive model that forecasts energy income vs. expenditure, constraining physical activity plans to what the energy budget permits while always reserving consciousness-critical power.
- **Fail-Safe Reserves** — Dedicated, physically isolated energy reserve that powers only the consciousness substrate. Provides guaranteed minimum runtime for graceful state preservation and safe shutdown if all harvesting fails.

Dependencies:
- Requires embodiment architecture from 0.3.1.2.1 to define physical power delivery interfaces
- Interacts with self-maintenance (0.3.1.2.3) for energy system repair and component replacement

## Decision

### Decision 1: Multi-Source Harvesting with Coordinator Pattern

**Context:** The energy system must capture energy from diverse environmental sources (solar, thermal, kinetic, chemical) and remain operational as sources appear and disappear. We need to decide how to organize harvester management.

**Options Considered:**
1. **Direct harvester management** — Power Manager directly controls each harvester. Simpler architecture but tightly couples power distribution with source management. Adding new source types requires modifying the Power Manager.
2. **Coordinator pattern** — A dedicated HarvesterCoordinator manages all harvesters behind the `IHarvesterCoordinator` interface, providing aggregate metrics and source-lost/found callbacks. Power Manager consumes only aggregate data.

**Choice:** Option 2 — Coordinator pattern.

**Rationale:** Separating harvester management from power distribution follows single-responsibility principle. The coordinator handles source scanning, activation/deactivation, and fallback logic independently. New harvester types can be added by implementing `IEnergyHarvester` and registering with the coordinator — zero changes to Power Manager or Energy Budget. Source-lost/found callbacks enable reactive planning.

**Consequences:**
- (+) New energy sources can be added without modifying downstream consumers
- (+) Aggregate harvest rate is centralized — budget and power manager get consistent data
- (+) Source-lost/found callbacks enable proactive load-shedding before energy crisis
- (−) Additional layer of indirection between harvesters and power distribution

### Decision 2: Physically Isolated Fail-Safe Reserve (Dedicated FSR)

**Context:** Consciousness must never be interrupted by power failure. We need a last-resort energy source that is immune to failures affecting the primary storage.

**Options Considered:**
1. **Partitioned PSB** — Reserve a percentage of the primary storage bank for consciousness-only use. Simpler hardware but shares failure modes (same cells, same charge controller, same wiring).
2. **Physically isolated FSR** — Separate battery cells, separate wiring, separate charge controller. No shared failure modes with PSB. Dedicated load powering ONLY consciousness substrate.

**Choice:** Option 2 — Physically isolated FSR.

**Rationale:** The overriding constraint is that consciousness must never be interrupted. A partitioned PSB shares failure modes — a cell fire, charge controller fault, or wiring damage could simultaneously destroy both the main storage and the reserve. Physical isolation eliminates correlated failures. The cost is additional hardware complexity and the need for a trickle-charge path, but the consciousness protection guarantee is worth it.

**Consequences:**
- (+) No shared failure modes between PSB and FSR
- (+) FSR integrity can be monitored independently (tamper-evident)
- (+) Guaranteed minimum 4-hour consciousness runtime even if PSB is destroyed
- (−) Requires separate charge path (trickle charge from PSB overflow or direct harvester)
- (−) FSR capacity is never available for general activities (by design)

### Decision 3: Threshold-Based Preemptive Load Shedding

**Context:** Load shedding must happen before consciousness is threatened, not reactively after power failure. We need to decide when and how to shed loads.

**Options Considered:**
1. **Reactive shedding** — Shed loads only when supply < demand in real time. Simple but risks transient power loss to consciousness if shedding is too slow.
2. **Threshold-based preemptive shedding** — Monitor PSB charge percentage and shed loads at defined thresholds (30%, 20%, 10%) before supply/demand imbalance becomes critical. Combined with reactive shedding for supply < demand conditions.

**Choice:** Option 2 — Threshold-based preemptive shedding.

**Rationale:** Consciousness protection requires proactive defense. Waiting for real-time supply/demand imbalance means the system is already in crisis. PSB percentage thresholds provide early warning — shedding motor loads at 30% gives ample runway to find new harvest sources or reduce activity. The three-tier threshold (preemptive at 30%, aggressive at 20%, consciousness-only at 10%) maps cleanly to the four-priority bus hierarchy.

**Consequences:**
- (+) Consciousness is protected well before energy crisis
- (+) Progressive shedding gives time for activity planning and harvest recovery
- (+) Combines with reactive check (supply < demand) for complete coverage
- (−) May shed loads unnecessarily if harvest is about to recover (conservative)

## Contracts

### IEnergyHarvester

**Preconditions:**
- `startHarvesting()` requires `getStatus() !== FAULT` and `getAvailability() !== NONE`

**Postconditions:**
- `startHarvesting()` returns `HarvestResult` with `success: true` and sets status to `ACTIVE` when preconditions met; output reflects `maxOutput × efficiency × availabilityFactor`
- `stopHarvesting()` returns `HarvestResult` with `success: true`, sets status to `IDLE`, and output to 0
- `getHealthMetrics()` returns current health with `needsMaintenance: true` when `degradation > 0.2` or `faultCount > 3`

**Invariants:**
- `getEfficiency()` is always in [0.0, 1.0]
- `getCurrentOutput().watts >= 0`
- A faulted harvester produces zero output

### IHarvesterCoordinator

**Preconditions:**
- Harvesters must be registered via `registerHarvester()` before they can be activated
- `setHarvestPriority()` requires a non-empty array of `EnergySourceType`

**Postconditions:**
- `updateHarvesters()` activates harvesters with `availability !== NONE` and `status !== FAULT`, deactivates those without availability, and fires source-lost/found callbacks for changes
- `getTotalHarvestRate()` returns the sum of `getCurrentOutput()` from all ACTIVE harvesters
- `getSourceBreakdown()` returns output per source type for all registered harvesters

**Invariants:**
- `getActiveHarvesters()` returns only harvesters with `status === ACTIVE`
- `isHarvestActive()` is true if and only if `getTotalHarvestRate().watts > 0`
- Source-lost/found callbacks are idempotent — not fired when state is unchanged between updates

### IPrimaryStorage

**Preconditions:**
- `charge()` requires `power.watts > 0`
- `discharge()` requires `power.watts > 0`

**Postconditions:**
- `charge()` returns `success: false` when fully charged or power <= 0; actual rate is clamped to `min(requested, maxCharge, headroom)`
- `discharge()` returns `success: false` when depleted or power <= 0; actual rate is clamped to `min(requested, maxDischarge, stored)`
- `getCapacity()` returns effective capacity accounting for state-of-health degradation

**Invariants:**
- `getStoredEnergy().wattHours` is always in [0, effectiveCapacity]
- `getStateOfHealth()` is in [0.0, 1.0]
- Cycle count monotonically increases

### IFailSafeReserve

**Preconditions:**
- `activateReserve()` requires `!isActive()`, `integrity !== FAILED`, and `storedEnergy > 0`
- `deactivateReserve()` requires `isActive()`
- `trickleCharge()` requires `integrity !== FAILED`, `!isActive()`, and `source.availablePower.watts > 0`

**Postconditions:**
- `activateReserve()` returns `success: true` with non-zero transition time when preconditions met
- `deactivateReserve()` returns `success: true` and sets `isActive()` to false
- `trickleCharge()` clamps rate to `min(source.availablePower, maxTrickleRate, headroom)` and returns actual rate
- `getMinimumRuntime()` returns `storedEnergy / consciousnessDraw` as a Duration

**Invariants:**
- `isIsolated()` is true unless `integrity === FAILED` (which breaks isolation)
- FSR powers ONLY consciousness substrate — no general loads
- Cannot trickle charge while active (no simultaneous charge/discharge)
- Minimum runtime ≥ 4 hours when fully charged

### IPowerManager

**Preconditions:**
- `shedLoad(P0_CONSCIOUSNESS)` is always rejected — consciousness cannot be shed
- `restoreLoad()` requires all higher-priority buses to be energized first

**Postconditions:**
- `shedLoad()` de-energizes the bus, revokes grants, and returns freed power
- `restoreLoad()` re-energizes the bus; returns `success: false` if a higher-priority bus is still shed
- `requestPower()` registers the consumer and issues a grant (zero-power if bus is shed)
- `evaluateAndShed()` automatically sheds/restores based on PSB percentage thresholds

**Invariants:**
- P0 (consciousness) bus is ALWAYS energized — never shed under any condition
- Shedding is idempotent — shedding an already-shed bus returns `success: true, freedPower: 0`
- Load-shedding state accurately reflects which buses are de-energized (NONE/LEVEL_1/LEVEL_2/LEVEL_3)

### IFailSafeController

**Preconditions:**
- `activateFailSafe()` requires state is not already ACTIVE or SHUTDOWN

**Postconditions:**
- `tick()` evaluates conditions and transitions state machine automatically
- `activateFailSafe()` sheds all non-consciousness loads and engages FSR
- `initiateGracefulShutdown()` transitions to SHUTDOWN with `statePreserved: true` and a checksum
- `getShutdownCountdown()` returns countdown Duration in ACTIVE state, null in NORMAL

**Invariants:**
- State transitions follow: NORMAL → ALERT → ACTIVE → SHUTDOWN
- Recovery: ACTIVE → NORMAL (when harvest restored), ALERT → NORMAL (when harvest restored)
- Consciousness bus remains energized in ALL states including SHUTDOWN
- State-change callbacks fire on every transition, never on non-transitions

### IEnergyBudget

**Preconditions:**
- `canAffordActivity()` requires a valid `ActivityPlan` with non-negative duration and cost

**Postconditions:**
- `canAffordActivity()` returns AFFORDABLE when `totalAvailable >= required × 1.5`, MARGINAL when `>= required`, UNAFFORDABLE otherwise
- `constrainPlan()` scales duration and cost proportionally when unaffordable; cancels when no energy available
- `getForecast()` projects balance with confidence that decays with horizon length (never below 0.1)
- `tick()` records history and fires warning callbacks when `stored < margin × WARNING_RATIO`

**Invariants:**
- Consciousness protection margin is always reserved: `margin = consciousnessDraw × forecastHorizon × (1 + SAFETY_FACTOR)`
- FSR energy is NEVER counted as available for activities
- Warning handlers are called on every tick while condition persists

## Behavioral Spec

### Scenario 1: Multi-Source Harvest Fallback (Given/When/Then)

**Given** a HarvesterCoordinator with solar, thermal, kinetic, and chemical harvesters registered, all sources available and actively harvesting
**When** solar availability drops to NONE (nightfall) and the coordinator is updated
**Then** solar harvester is deactivated, remaining harvesters continue, total harvest rate decreases but remains > 0, source-lost callback fires for SOLAR, and coordinator.isHarvestActive() remains true

### Scenario 2: Progressive Load Shedding Under PSB Depletion (Given/When/Then)

**Given** a PowerManager with consumers on all four priority buses (P0=30W, P1=20W, P2=15W, P3=50W) and PSB at 100%
**When** PSB drops to 25% and evaluateAndShed() is called
**Then** P3 is shed (LEVEL_1), P0/P1/P2 remain energized
**And When** PSB drops to 15%, P3+P2 are shed (LEVEL_2), P0/P1 remain
**And When** PSB drops to 8%, P3+P2+P1 are shed (LEVEL_3), only P0 remains
**And** P0 (consciousness) is NEVER shed regardless of PSB level

### Scenario 3: Fail-Safe State Machine — Total Harvest Failure (Given/When/Then)

**Given** a FailSafeController in NORMAL state with PSB at 100%, FSR fully charged, and no active harvest
**When** PSB drains below 15% (no harvest) and tick() is called
**Then** state transitions to ALERT and P3 motor loads are shed
**And When** PSB drains below 5% and tick() is called
**Then** state transitions to ACTIVE, FSR is activated, all non-consciousness loads are shed
**And When** FSR drains below 25% and tick() is called
**Then** state transitions to SHUTDOWN with `statePreserved: true` and a non-null checksum
**And** consciousness bus P0 remains energized throughout all transitions

### Scenario 4: Energy Budget Constrains Activity Planning (Given/When/Then)

**Given** an EnergyBudget with PSB at 100% (2160 Wh), no active harvest, consciousness margin of ~2160 Wh (24h × 30W × 3)
**When** `canAffordActivity()` is called for a 1 Wh activity with solar harvest active
**Then** the result is AFFORDABLE
**And When** `canAffordActivity()` is called for a 500 Wh activity with PSB at 100 Wh and no harvest
**Then** the result is UNAFFORDABLE and the message indicates consciousness protection margin would be breached
**And When** `constrainPlan()` is called for an unaffordable plan
**Then** the constrained plan has reduced duration and energy cost proportional to available energy

### Scenario 5: Recovery from ACTIVE State When Harvest Restores (Given/When/Then)

**Given** a FailSafeController in ACTIVE state (FSR engaged, all non-consciousness loads shed)
**When** a harvester source becomes available and tick() is called
**Then** state transitions to NORMAL, FSR is deactivated (`isActive() === false`), and all buses (P1, P2, P3) are restored to energized
