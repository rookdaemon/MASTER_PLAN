# 0.4.2.2 Stellar Resource Extraction [DONE]

## Description

C2.2 from the Master Plan. Autonomous harvesting of energy and materials from stars and planetary bodies without human oversight.

This provides the raw material and energy foundation for self-replication at each destination. The system forms an integrated pipeline: harvest stellar energy → prospect and mine bodies → refine into fabrication-ready materials — all operating autonomously.

### Key Subsystems

- **Stellar energy harvesting:** Deployable collector arrays (partial Dyson swarm or focused reflector/photovoltaic units) that tap stellar output to provide industrial-scale power at each destination system.
- **Prospecting and mining:** Autonomous survey of local bodies (asteroids, moons, planetesimals) to identify extractable metals, semiconductors, volatiles, and rare elements; robotic mining operations to retrieve them.
- **Material processing and refining:** In-situ refinement chain converting raw regolith/ore into purified feedstocks (structural metals, silicon, carbon composites, etc.) suitable for probe fabrication by 0.4.2.4 replication protocols.
- **Autonomous operation and adaptation:** Closed-loop control with no external commands or resupply. The system must characterize unknown stellar/planetary compositions on arrival and reconfigure extraction parameters accordingly.

### Dependencies

- Can be designed in parallel with 0.4.2.3 (propulsion)
- Feeds into 0.4.2.1 (probe architectures) and 0.4.2.4 (replication protocols)
- Builds on 0.4.1 asteroid resource utilization (C1.2) and autonomous space manufacturing (C1.3)

## Decision — Extraction Pipeline Architecture

### Context
The extraction system must convert raw stellar/planetary resources into fabrication-ready feedstocks autonomously. The design must decide: how to structure the pipeline (monolith vs. modular), what collector technology to use, and how to handle unknown compositions.

### Options Considered
1. **Monolithic pipeline** — single integrated system handles energy, mining, refining in tightly-coupled sequence. Simpler control, but brittle to partial failures and hard to adapt per-subsystem.
2. **Modular pipeline with autonomous subsystems** — four decoupled subsystems (SEH, PM, MPR, AOA) with well-defined interfaces. Each subsystem independently adaptable; mission planner orchestrates.
3. **Swarm-based fully distributed** — every unit is a generalist that can harvest, mine, and refine. Maximum redundancy but extreme complexity and lower efficiency per unit.

### Choice
**Option 2: Modular pipeline with autonomous subsystems.**

### Rationale
- Matches the natural decomposition of the problem (energy → extraction → refining → control).
- Decoupled interfaces (`EnergyBudget`, `RawMaterialManifest`, `FeedstockInventory`, `ReplicationReadinessSignal`) allow each subsystem to be adapted to local conditions independently.
- Compositional adaptation engine can modify refining recipes without affecting mining or energy subsystems.
- Aligns with bootstrap sequence: each subsystem comes online in order, and earlier subsystems need not be redesigned when later ones activate.

### Consequences
- Requires well-defined inter-subsystem contracts (see Contracts below).
- Mission planner (AOA) must be sophisticated enough to orchestrate four subsystems and handle cross-subsystem failures.
- Testing requires mocking subsystem boundaries.

## Decision — Compositional Adaptation Strategy

### Context
Destination systems will have unknown stellar types and planetary mineralogies. The refining pipeline must handle compositions never seen during design.

### Options Considered
1. **Fixed recipe library only** — carry a large library of pre-computed refining recipes indexed by composition. Fails on truly novel compositions.
2. **First-principles synthesis only** — derive refining parameters from physics/chemistry models at runtime. Computationally expensive and risky without validation.
3. **Library-first with first-principles fallback** — search recipe library first; if no match, synthesize from models; always validate on small batch before full-scale commitment.

### Choice
**Option 3: Library-first with first-principles fallback.**

### Rationale
- Library lookup is fast and reliable for common compositions (covers majority of cases).
- First-principles fallback handles edge cases without requiring an exhaustive library.
- Small-batch validation prevents catastrophic failures from untested processes.
- `AdaptationLog` provides auditability for downstream replication protocols (C2.4).

### Consequences
- Must carry both a recipe library and first-principles chemistry/physics models.
- Small-batch validation adds latency to adaptation; acceptable given decade-scale timeline.
- `adaptRefiningForComposition()` implements the degradation path when processes are unavailable.

## Contracts

### StellarCharacterization
- **Preconditions:** `spectralClass` ∈ {F, G, K, M}; `subType` ∈ [0, 9]; `luminosity_solar` > 0; `variabilityIndex` ∈ [0, 1].
- **Postconditions:** `temperature_K` computed from spectral class and sub-type; all fields populated.
- **Invariants:** Temperature falls within the range for the given spectral class.

### EnergyBudget
- **Preconditions:** `totalOutput_watts` ≥ 0.
- **Postconditions:** Sum of allocations (mining + refining + fabrication + computation) ≤ totalOutput × (1 − MIN_RESERVE_MARGIN). `reserveMarginFraction` ≥ MIN_RESERVE_MARGIN.
- **Invariants:** Allocations are non-negative; allocations sum to ≤ totalOutput.

### ResourceSurveyResult
- **Preconditions:** At least one `CelestialBody` with a `CompositionAssay` provided.
- **Postconditions:** `rankedBodies` sorted by extraction priority (descending); `allClassesCoverable` true iff the union of all assayed bodies covers all required `MaterialClass` values.
- **Invariants:** Every body in `rankedBodies` has a corresponding assay.

### FeedstockInventory
- **Preconditions:** `entries` is a list of `FeedstockEntry` with non-negative mass and purity ∈ [0, 1].
- **Postconditions:** `completionByClass` maps each required class to its completion fraction ∈ [0, 1].
- **Invariants:** Completion fraction = min(1.0, actual_mass / required_mass) for each class.

### ReplicationReadinessSignal
- **Preconditions:** Valid `FeedstockInventory` and `FeedstockSpec`.
- **Postconditions:** `ready` = true iff every class in spec has sufficient mass at required purity. `classCoverage` map has one entry per spec requirement.
- **Invariants:** `ready` is logically equivalent to all values in `classCoverage` being true.

### AdaptationLogEntry
- **Preconditions:** `timestamp_years` ≥ 0; `category` is a valid enum value.
- **Postconditions:** Entry is immutable once created.
- **Invariants:** `validated` starts false; set true only after small-batch validation succeeds.

## Behavioral Spec — Bootstrap Sequence

### Scenario 1: Nominal bootstrap in Sun-like system
```
Given an arriving probe enters a G2V system with rocky bodies
When the probe deploys seed collectors (Phase 1, Year 0–1)
  And lands mining units on the highest-priority asteroid (Phase 2, Year 1–3)
  And fabricates additional collectors and mining units (Phase 3, Year 3–10)
  And reaches full-scale refining operations (Phase 4, Year 10–20)
Then ReplicationReadiness reaches 1.0 within MAX_REPLICATION_TIMELINE_YEARS
  And all MaterialClass entries in FeedstockSpec are covered at required purity
  And ReplicationReadinessSignal.ready is true
```

### Scenario 2: Compositional adaptation for novel mineralogy
```
Given a refining subsystem encounters a material composition not in its recipe library
When it searches the recipe library and finds no match
  And synthesizes new refining parameters from first-principles models
  And validates the new process on a small batch
Then an AdaptationLogEntry is created with validated=true
  And the adapted recipe is used for full-scale refining
  And output purity is ≤ the default recipe purity (degraded but functional)
```

### Scenario 3: Red dwarf system with limited energy
```
Given an arriving probe enters an M5V system (luminosity ≈ 0.0017 L☉)
When estimateCollectorsForTarget is computed for TARGET_FULL_SCALE_POWER_WATTS
Then the required collector count is orders of magnitude higher than for a G-type star
  And the bootstrap timeline extends but remains within MAX_REPLICATION_TIMELINE_YEARS
  And energy budget allocation automatically reduces all subsystem allocations proportionally
```

## Acceptance Criteria
- **Energy sufficiency:** Stellar collector architecture harvests enough energy to power all mining, refining, fabrication, and computation for a complete probe replication cycle. Verified by `isEnergySufficient()` returning true for reference feedstock specs at TARGET_FULL_SCALE_POWER_WATTS.
- **Material completeness:** Mining and refining pipeline produces all material classes needed for probe fabrication (structural metals, semiconductors, thermal management, propellant precursors) from local resources. Verified by `evaluateReplicationReadiness()` with `ready=true` when all 6 `MaterialClass` entries meet spec.
- **Closed-loop autonomy:** Entire extraction pipeline — from stellar collector deployment through refined feedstock output — operates without human oversight, remote commands, or resupply from origin civilization. Verified by isolation simulation: no external inputs post-arrival.
- **Throughput:** Resource extraction and processing rate is sufficient to produce a complete probe's worth of fabrication-ready materials within MAX_REPLICATION_TIMELINE_YEARS (50 years). Verified by `validateBootstrapTimeline()`.
- **Compositional adaptability:** System autonomously surveys destination system bodies, characterizes available compositions, and reconfigures extraction/refining parameters for stellar types F through M and varying planetary mineralogies. Verified by `adaptRefiningForComposition()` producing valid adapted recipes and `evaluateSurvey()` correctly handling parametric sweeps.
- **Bootstrap capability:** Initial seed package (carried by arriving probe) is minimal — the extraction system bootstraps full-scale operations from a compact deployable kit. Verified by `validateSeedPackageFraction()` with seed mass ≤ MAX_SEED_FRACTION of final infrastructure mass.
