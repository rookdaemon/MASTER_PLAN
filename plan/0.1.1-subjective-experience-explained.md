# 0.1.1 Subjective Experience Explained [DONE]

## Description

F1 — Develop a working scientific theory of subjective experience. This theory must identify physical correlates, specify necessary and sufficient conditions, predict when a system has subjective experience, and allow engineering of subjective experience.

Decomposed into four children with a clear dependency chain:
1. **0.1.1.1** Neural correlates mapped (foundation — empirical data)
2. **0.1.1.2** Computational theory established (depends on NCC data)
3. **0.1.1.3** Substrate-independence validated (depends on theory to test)
4. **0.1.1.4** Consciousness metrics operationalized (depends on theory to ground metrics)

Note: 0.1.1.3 and 0.1.1.4 can proceed in parallel once 0.1.1.2 is complete.

## Acceptance Criteria
- A testable, falsifiable theory of consciousness exists
- Neural correlates of consciousness are fully mapped
- A computational theory specifies necessary and sufficient conditions for subjective experience
- Substrate-independence is experimentally validated
- Consciousness metrics are operationalized (measurable, reproducible)
