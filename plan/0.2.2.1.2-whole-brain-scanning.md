# 0.2.2.1.2 Whole-Brain Scanning [DONE]

## Description

Develop technology capable of scanning a complete biological brain at the resolution determined by 0.2.2.1.1, ideally without destroying the original.

### Key challenges
- Non-destructive scanning at cellular or sub-cellular resolution
- Capturing dynamic state (not just static structure) — synaptic weights, neurotransmitter levels, ongoing activity patterns
- Handling the scale: ~86 billion neurons, ~100 trillion synapses in a human brain
- Data acquisition speed vs. brain state drift during scanning
- Storage and representation of the resulting dataset

## Acceptance Criteria
- Scanning modality selected and justified against the fidelity level specified by 0.2.2.1.1 (e.g. advanced MRI, electron microscopy, optical clearing, or novel physics)
- Non-destructive scanning demonstrated on at least one whole brain, OR formal proof that destruction is unavoidable at the required fidelity (with minimally-destructive alternative specified)
- Static structural data captured: neuron positions, morphology, and connectivity at required resolution
- Dynamic state data captured: synaptic weights, neurotransmitter concentrations, and ion channel states at a snapshot sufficient for emulation initialization
- Scan completion time bounded such that brain-state drift during acquisition remains below a defined error tolerance
- Output data format specified and compatible with the simulation pipeline of 0.2.2.1.3
- Complete scan of at least one biological brain stored in computationally usable format with documented schema
- Scan accuracy validated against independent ground-truth measurements at the required resolution
