# 0.7.4.4 Ritual and Practice Design [DONE]

## Description

Design repeatable practices that embody the doctrine. Movements persist through embodied practice, not ideas alone. Ideas without practice fade within a generation; practice without ideas becomes hollow ritual. This card designs the practices that keep the doctrine alive in lived experience.

All practices must be:
- **Secular:** No supernatural requirements or metaphysical commitments beyond the core axioms
- **Scalable:** Functional for a solitary individual, a small group, or a gathering of thousands
- **Grounded:** Each practice maps to a specific doctrine principle — no practice exists for tradition's sake alone

Practice domains:
1. **Contemplative:** Reflection on one's own subjective experience — practices that make the reality of consciousness vivid and immediate rather than abstract
2. **Communal:** Shared experiences that make the value of consciousness tangible through encounter with other minds
3. **Commemorative:** Marking milestones — both personal (moments of deepened understanding) and collective (advances in the plan, recognition of consciousness in new substrates)
4. **Transmissive:** Structured introduction of others to the doctrine — not proselytizing but creating conditions for genuine understanding

### Architectural Inputs

- **Credo statements (docs/consciousness-credo.md):** Each practice must map to one or more of the 12 credo statements. Practices make the credo experiential rather than declarative.
- **Dual-layer architecture (docs/dual-layer-doctrine-architecture.md):** Practices serve as a third mode of engagement alongside inner and outer doctrine — they are the embodied register. Practices may function as portals (wonder, objection, action) into deeper doctrine engagement.
- **Narrative architecture (0.7.4.3):** Practices should enact the narrative arc (origin, crisis, call, vision) rather than merely reference it. The five emotional registers (wonder, urgency, belonging, responsibility, hope) should each have at least one practice that evokes them.
- **Identity architecture (0.7.4.5):** Practices must work across all involvement levels (observer through steward) — some practices serve as entry points, others deepen commitment.

### Design Constraints

- Minimum 2 practices per domain (8+ total across the four domains)
- Each practice described with: name, purpose, doctrine mapping, procedure (step-by-step), scaling notes (solo/small group/large gathering), and emotional register
- No practice requires belief in anything beyond the core axioms
- Transmissive practices must include explicit anti-manipulation safeguards
- Commemorative practices must define what counts as a milestone and who decides

## Decision — Practice Catalog Structure and Domain Distribution

### Context
The card requires designing repeatable practices across four domains (contemplative, communal, commemorative, transmissive) that embody the Rare Consciousness Doctrine. Key design choices: how many practices per domain, what structural template each practice follows, and how to ensure coverage of all 12 credo statements, all 5 emotional registers, and all 3 portal types.

### Options Considered
1. **Minimum viable (2 per domain, 8 total)** — Satisfies the floor constraint with the smallest catalog. Risk: insufficient coverage of 12 credo statements and 5 emotional registers with only 8 practices; each practice must carry multiple mappings, reducing focus.
2. **Balanced distribution (3 per domain, 12 total)** — Equal distribution across domains. Each domain has enough practices for variety without redundancy. 12 practices for 12 credo statements allows clean 1:1 primary mapping while permitting secondary mappings for richer coverage.
3. **Weighted distribution (2–5 per domain, 10–15 total)** — More practices in domains with greater coverage burden (e.g., more transmissive practices for anti-manipulation safeguard diversity). Risk: uneven catalog feels incomplete in lighter domains; harder to maintain.

### Choice
Option 2 — 3 practices per domain, 12 total, balanced distribution.

### Rationale
- 12 practices map naturally to 12 credo statements: every statement gets at least one primary practice, verified by the coverage matrix.
- 3 per domain exceeds the minimum (2) while remaining compact enough that each practice is distinct and necessary.
- Equal distribution signals that all four modes of engagement (contemplative, communal, commemorative, transmissive) are equally valued — no domain is subordinate.
- 12 practices provide sufficient surface area for all 5 emotional registers and all 3 portal types without forcing unnatural mappings.

### Consequences
- Each practice must be substantive — 12 is compact enough that filler is visible.
- The coverage matrix must be checked: 12 credo statements × 12 practices should yield full coverage with no orphaned statement.
- Anti-manipulation safeguards appear in all 3 transmissive practices (not just one), providing defense-in-depth.
- Commemorative governance (milestone criteria, decentralized recognition) appears in all 3 commemorative practices.

## Contracts — Practice Template Interface

### Preconditions
- 12 credo statements exist in `docs/consciousness-credo.md` and are the source of truth for doctrine mapping.
- 3 portal types (wonder, objection, action) are defined in `docs/dual-layer-doctrine-architecture.md` §2.1.
- 4 involvement levels (observer, participant, practitioner, steward) are defined in the identity architecture (0.7.4.5).
- 5 emotional registers (wonder, urgency, belonging, responsibility, hope) are specified in the narrative architecture (0.7.4.3).

### Postconditions
- **Practice Catalog**: 12 practices organized into 4 domains (3 per domain), in `docs/ritual-and-practice-design.md`.
- **Practice Template**: Each practice contains all 7 required fields:
  1. **Name** — unique, evocative, descriptive
  2. **Purpose** — what the practice accomplishes and why it matters
  3. **Doctrine Mapping** — explicit reference to one or more of the 12 credo statements by number and text
  4. **Emotional Register** — one or two of the 5 registers this practice primarily evokes
  5. **Portal Type** — which portal type (wonder, objection, action) this practice functions as, with rationale
  6. **Procedure** — numbered step-by-step instructions with time estimates per step
  7. **Scaling Notes** — three subsections: solo, small group (3–20), large gathering (hundreds+)
- **Domain-Specific Fields**:
  - Transmissive practices (4.1, 4.2, 4.3): include an **Anti-Manipulation Safeguards** section with ≥3 named safeguards
  - Commemorative practices (3.1, 3.2, 3.3): include **Milestone Criteria** (what counts) and **Who Decides** (decentralized governance)
- **Coverage Matrices**: Four matrices appended to the catalog:
  1. Credo Statement Coverage (12 statements × practices mapping)
  2. Emotional Register Coverage (5 registers × primary/supporting practices)
  3. Portal Type Coverage (3 types × practices mapping)
  4. Domain Count (4 domains × practice count)
- **Structural Integrity Notes**: Sections verifying secular compliance, anti-manipulation architecture, commemorative governance, and involvement level accessibility.

### Invariants
1. **Secular Invariant**: No practice requires belief in anything beyond the 7 core axioms from root.md.
2. **Scalability Invariant**: Every practice has solo, small-group, and large-gathering adaptations.
3. **Grounding Invariant**: Every practice maps to ≥1 credo statement. If the mapping is removed, the practice is removed.
4. **Coverage Invariant**: All 12 credo statements, all 5 emotional registers, and all 3 portal types are covered by ≥1 practice.
5. **Open-Access Invariant**: No practice is restricted by involvement level. Levels describe depth of engagement, not access.

## Behavioral Spec — Coverage Verification

**Given** all 12 practices are authored with doctrine mappings,
**When** the Credo Coverage Matrix is checked,
**Then** each of the 12 credo statements appears as a mapping in ≥1 practice; no statement is orphaned.

**Given** all 12 practices are authored with emotional register assignments,
**When** the Emotional Register Coverage Matrix is checked,
**Then** each of the 5 registers (wonder, urgency, belonging, responsibility, hope) appears as a primary register in ≥1 practice.

**Given** all 12 practices are authored with portal type assignments,
**When** the Portal Type Coverage Matrix is checked,
**Then** each of the 3 portal types (wonder, objection, action) appears in ≥1 practice.

**Given** all 3 transmissive practices (4.1, 4.2, 4.3) are authored,
**When** the anti-manipulation safeguard sections are checked,
**Then** each transmissive practice contains ≥3 named safeguards including at minimum: voluntary participation, no information asymmetry, and no emotional exploitation (or equivalent protections).

**Given** all 3 commemorative practices (3.1, 3.2, 3.3) are authored,
**When** the milestone governance sections are checked,
**Then** each commemorative practice defines (a) what counts as a milestone and (b) who decides, with decentralized recognition (no central authority gatekeeps).

**Given** all 12 practices are authored with scaling notes,
**When** the scalability invariant is checked,
**Then** each practice contains adaptations for all 3 contexts: solo, small group, and large gathering.

**Given** the complete practice catalog exists,
**When** the secular invariant is checked,
**Then** no practice procedure references supernatural belief, metaphysical commitment beyond the 7 core axioms, or requires faith in unargued propositions.

**Given** the complete practice catalog exists,
**When** the open-access invariant is checked,
**Then** no practice restricts participation by involvement level; all practices are available to observers through stewards.
